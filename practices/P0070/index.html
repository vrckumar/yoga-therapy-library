Nadi Shuddhi

Nadi Shuddhi (Alternative Spelling — Cross-Reference to Nadi Suddhi)

This is an alternative transliteration entry. The practice documented here is identical to Nadi Suddhi (P0069). Both names refer to the same yogic practice: the alternate-nostril breathing (Anuloma Viloma) used to purify the energy channels (nadis) and balance the solar (Pingala) and lunar (Ida) flows of prana.

Transliteration Note

The Sanskrit nāḍī śuddhi (नाडी शुद्धि) is romanised in multiple ways across different SVYASA publications and PGDYTP course materials:

Spelling Source context
Nadi Suddhi Primary spelling in YIC Manual 2 and most PGDYTP 101 modules
Nadi Shuddhi Alternative romanisation — the "sh" in shuddhi reflects the retroflex ś sound
Nadishuddhi Single-word form used in some clinical documentation
Nadi Shodhana Alternative name used in some traditions (same technique)

Suddhi and Shuddhi are both acceptable romanisations of the Sanskrit śuddhi (purification). In the database, P0069 holds the primary entry; P0070 is the alternate-spelling cross-reference.

Summary of the Practice

See P0069-nadi-suddhi.md for the complete technical description.

In brief:

  • Alternate-nostril breathing using Nasagra Mudra (right thumb controls right nostril, ring and little finger control left nostril)
  • Standard ratio: 5:5 (inhale left for 5 counts, exhale right for 5 counts; inhale right for 5 counts, exhale left for 5 counts)
  • Standard prescription: 9 rounds per session, 2–3 minutes
  • Used in all eight IAYT protocols as the primary pranayama practice
