Spinal Twist

Spinal Twist (Kati Chakra — Twisting)

Standing dynamic spinal rotation — twisting the torso alternately to the right and left with arms swinging. The standing dynamic version of the spinal rotation in the loosening series. (Described in the source as "Twisting.") Similar to P0030 (Waist Rotation) but typically emphasising a greater amplitude of rotation and the forward reach of the leading arm.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana. Feet approximately one meter apart. Arms raised sideways at shoulder height.

Practice:

  1. Stand with feet one meter apart. Raise arms sideways (horizontal).
  2. Keep legs firm. Twist to the right — right arm extends to the right, left arm bends at the elbow to bring the hand toward the chest. Look toward the right hand's fingertips.
  3. Return to centre (inhale).
  4. Twist to the left in the same manner.
  5. Gradually increase speed to maximum. Perform 10–20 rounds.
  6. Slow down. Stop. Relax in Tadasana.

Note (from source): All twisting should be above the waist level. Keep the body below the waist straight and firm. Do not bend the knees.

Breathing: Exhale on each twist; inhale returning to centre.

Duration: 10–20 rounds.

2. Anatomical Activation

  • External and internal obliques: primary trunk rotators
  • Thoracic rotators (semispinalis, rotatores): segmental thoracic rotation
  • SCM and cervical rotators: neck follows the arm rotation
  • Thoracolumbar fascia: rotational tensioning
  • Rhomboids and serratus anterior: alternately retract and protract the scapulae with each twist

The emphasis on "above the waist" — with the lower body firm — ensures that the rotation is thoracic rather than lumbar, since the lumbar spine has very limited rotation range (approximately 5° per level vs the thoracic spine's 7° per level).

3. Physiological Effects

Thoracic rotation mobilisation — the primary clinical significance

The thoracic spine provides the majority of spinal rotation capacity. When thoracic rotation is restricted (common in sitting postures, kyphosis, and chest tightening), the lumbar spine is forced to provide compensatory rotation — far exceeding its safe range and contributing to lumbar injury. Standing spinal twist specifically exercises the thoracic rotational range, preventing this compensation.

Oblique and paraspinal balance

The obliques and paraspinal rotators are the muscles responsible for trunk rotation in all functional activities — throwing, turning, reaching across the body. Their bilateral, alternating activation in standing twist maintains the functional rotation capacity of the trunk.

Abdominal organ stimulation

The twisting compression and release of the abdominal contents — colonic massage — is a secondary benefit. Each twist compresses one side of the abdomen, applying peristaltic-like pressure to the ascending (right) or descending (left) colon.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — thoracic rotational mobilisation; oblique and paraspinal muscle conditioning; thoracolumbar fascia mobilisation.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the twisting movement is the most effective prana-activating motion for the thorax in the yogic tradition.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Back Pain (A004) Thoracic rotation mobilisation prevents lumbar compensatory overload
Constipation / GID Abdominal organ stimulation through alternating twisting compression
Diabetes (A001) Pancreatic stimulation through twisting
Obesity (A002) Oblique activation and waist mobilisation
All IAYT protocols Explicitly listed in PGDYTP 101 as standard loosening exercise

6. Contraindications

  • Acute lumbar disc herniation — avoid rotation in acute phase
  • Severe osteoporosis — reduce speed and range significantly; rotational vertebral fracture risk
  • Spondylolisthesis — avoid forced rotation at the listhesis level

7. Variations & Progressions

Standard (as described): Arms horizontal, thoracic emphasis.

Arms overhead (advanced): Greater rotational demand; also stretches the intercostals.

Sequencing context: Part of the trunk series (P0030–P0035). Progresses to P0035 (Side Bending).
