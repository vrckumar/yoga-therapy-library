Aswasanchalanasana

Aswasanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Aswa = horse, sanchalana = movement/gait. The equestrian (lunge) pose, so named because the body position resembles a horse rider with one knee bent and the torso upright. This is position 3 and position 8 (or 4 and 9 in the 10-step Surya Namaskar) in the classical Surya Namaskar sequence. The front hip is in deep flexion while the back hip is extended — producing a strong hip flexor stretch on the trailing leg and hip flexor activation on the leading leg. One of the few poses that creates simultaneous opposing stretch across both hips.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): As it occurs in Surya Namaskar Step 3 — from the forward bend (Padahastasana), palms flat on either side of the feet.

Practice (Step 3 of Surya Namaskar):

  1. From Padahastasana (forward fold, palms on the floor), inhale and kick (step) the right leg back, resting on the right toes.
  2. Push the buttocks forward and downward so that the left knee is bent at approximately 90° and the left thigh is approximately parallel to the floor. The left shin is vertical.
  3. Look upward — the cervical spine extends into a mild backbend.
  4. The palms remain flat on the floor on either side of the left foot throughout.
  5. The right leg is fully extended behind, hip in extension, right knee off the ground, weight on the right toes.
  6. Hold for the inhaled breath or maintain for the prescribed duration.

Step 8 (Return): The same posture is performed with the right leg forward and the left leg back. Inhale while bringing the right leg between the hands.

Duration: Held briefly within the Surya Namaskar flow; 5–10 seconds per side.

2. Anatomical Activation

Primary muscles engaged

  • Front leg (left in Step 3):

    • Quadriceps (rectus femoris, vastus group): control the bent-knee lunge position
    • Gluteus maximus of the front leg: works to prevent the knee from caving inward

  • Back leg (right in Step 3):

    • Hip flexors (iliopsoas, rectus femoris): strongly stretched as the right hip extends and the right knee is off the floor — this is the primary therapeutic stretch of the posture
    • Quadriceps of the back leg: contracted to keep the knee straight
    • Gastrocnemius and soleus: active on the back foot's toes

  • Spine and trunk:

    • Thoracic and lumbar erector spinae: maintain the upright spine and cervical extension (gaze upward)
    • Anterior abdominal wall: eccentrically stretched in the lunge position

Hip flexor stretch mechanism

The right (back) iliopsoas is under maximum stretch when:

  • The right hip is in full extension
  • The right knee is raised off the floor (no ground support)
  • The pelvis is driven forward ("push the buttocks forward and downward")

This is the deepest hip flexor stretch achievable in the Surya Namaskar sequence and directly addresses the psoas shortening caused by prolonged sitting.

3. Physiological Effects

Hip flexor lengthening and pelvic tilt correction

The iliopsoas is the most commonly shortened muscle in sedentary populations. Chronic shortening of the psoas:

  • Creates anterior pelvic tilt
  • Compresses the lumbar intervertebral spaces
  • Contributes to chronic low back pain

Aswasanchalanasana applies a sustained stretch to the iliopsoas in its most lengthened position (hip extended, knee off floor), directly reversing this pattern. Performed bilaterally (both steps 3 and 8 of Surya Namaskar), it provides the body's most accessible daily hip flexor mobilisation.

Quadriceps and anterior thigh strengthening

The front-leg quadriceps work concentrically to hold the lunge. The deeper the lunge (front thigh parallel to floor), the greater the eccentric demand on the quadriceps. This provides progressive lower limb strengthening relevant to knee rehabilitation and weight management.

Spinal extension activation

The instruction to "arch the back concave upwards" and look upward activates the thoracic and cervical extensors — complementing the hip flexor stretch with a full anterior-chain opening from ankle to throat.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — hip flexor stretching, quadriceps strengthening, spinal extension.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the deep forward step with open chest is associated with vigorous prana vayu activation; stimulates Manipura (solar plexus) and Anahata (heart) energy centres.

5. Position in Surya Namaskar

Step Side Action
Step 3 Right leg back Inhale; right hip flexor stretched
Step 8 Right leg forward (left back) Inhale; left hip flexor stretched

Bilaterally symmetrical stretch achieved over one complete round of Surya Namaskar.

6. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Back Pain (A004) Hip flexor stretching corrects anterior pelvic tilt; reduces lumbar compression
Obesity (A002) Dynamic lower body strengthening in the Surya Namaskar sequence
Diabetes Mellitus (A001) Large muscle group activation; improves peripheral glucose utilisation
General fitness (all protocols) Component of the Surya Namaskar warm-up

7. Contraindications

  • Acute knee injury (front knee): deep lunge flexion (90°) may not be appropriate; perform a shallower lunge
  • Severe hip arthritis: modify the depth of the lunge
  • Wrist injury: weight is on the palms; use fist support or reduce time in position
  • Pregnancy (later stages): lunge depth should be reduced; balance assistance may be needed
