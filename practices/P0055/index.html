Alternate Foot Knee Lumbar Stretch

Alternate Foot Knee Lumbar Stretch (Padartha Janu Prishtha Naman)

Alternating heel-to-knee diagonal rotation — each foot placed alternately on the opposite knee, with the leg then falling to the side. Combines the hip opening of Half Butterfly (P0046) with the rotational element of lumbar stretching (P0053–P0054) in a flowing, alternating sequence.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Supine Posture. Both legs extended.

Practice:

  1. Bend the right knee; place the right foot on the left knee.
  2. Allow the right knee to drop to the right — external rotation of the right hip. The left leg remains extended.
  3. Using the left hand, optionally guide the right knee further toward the right floor.
  4. Hold for 3–5 breaths.
  5. Return the right foot to the floor. Extend the right leg.
  6. Repeat on the left.
  7. Alternate 5–10 rounds.

Breathing: Exhale on the knee drop; breathe normally during hold; inhale returning to centre.

2. Anatomical Activation

  • Hip external rotators (ipsilateral): elongated in the external rotation drop
  • Adductors (ipsilateral): stretched in the abduction component
  • Lumbar rotators: mildly engaged in the diagonal stretch
  • Thoracolumbar fascia: lateral and rotational

3. Physiological Effects

Combined hip opening and lumbar rotation

This practice combines two previously practiced effects in a flowing alternating pattern:

  • The foot-on-knee position opens the hip externally (as in Half Butterfly, P0046)
  • The knee drop to the side creates a mild lumbar rotation (less intense than P0053–P0054)

The combination and alternating nature makes this practice particularly good as a flowing movement meditation — smooth, breath-synchronised, bilateral.

Adductor alternating stretch

The alternating left-right adductor stretch in this flowing pattern provides a rhythmic adductor mobilisation — more dynamic and less sustained than Full Butterfly, but covering the alternating movement coordination.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — combined hip external rotation and mild lumbar rotation; alternating adductor elongation; smooth sequential movement.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha / Manomaya Kosha — the alternating, breath-synchronised flow begins to approach the meditative movement quality of vinyasa.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Back Pain (A004) Flowing lumbar rotation with hip opening
Hip Tightness External rotator and adductor alternating mobilisation
General Stiffness Flowing alternating movement is accessible and comfortable
All IAYT protocols Connecting movement between hip opening and lumbar stretching sub-series

6. Contraindications

  • Acute hip pathology — reduce external rotation range
  • Acute lumbar disc herniation — reduce rotation range

7. Variations & Progressions

Without hand guidance (standard): Let gravity alone drop the knee.

With hand guidance (deeper): Gently guide the knee further toward the floor.

Sequencing context: Part of the combined hip-lumbar sub-series (P0046–P0056). Progresses to P0056 (Dorsal Stretch — prone).
