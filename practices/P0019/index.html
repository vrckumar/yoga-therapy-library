Loosening of Elbow

Loosening of Elbow (Kehuni Chakra)

Circumduction of the forearm/elbow complex — typically performed as full arm circles from the elbow pivot point, or as wrist-led circles with the elbow semi-fixed. This adds the rotational and multi-planar component to the sagittal-plane elbow bending (P0018).

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Standing or Seated. Arms by sides.

Practice — Form 1 (forearm circles with elbow fixed at 90°):

  1. Bend both elbows to 90°, upper arms close to the body.
  2. Move both forearms in large clockwise circles in the horizontal plane — the forearms describe a circle while the elbows remain relatively stationary at 90° flexion.
  3. Perform 10 rounds clockwise, then 10 anti-clockwise.

Practice — Form 2 (full arm swing from shoulder with elbow as pivot):

  1. Let arms hang loosely.
  2. Swing the forearms freely in large circles by allowing the elbow to flex and extend through the arc — a pendulum motion.
  3. 10 rounds each direction.

Breathing: Normal throughout.

Duration: 10 rounds each direction.

2. Anatomical Activation

Structures engaged during elbow circumduction

  • Pronator teres and pronator quadratus: forearm pronation component of the circle
  • Supinator and biceps brachii: forearm supination component
  • Brachioradialis: neutral forearm position; acts as a "shock absorber" during the swing
  • Proximal and distal radioulnar joints: forearm rotation axes — these joints specifically mobilised by the pronation-supination component

3. Physiological Effects

Radioulnar joint mobilisation — the rotational axis

The forearm rotation (pronation-supination) occurs at both the proximal radioulnar joint (at the elbow) and the distal radioulnar joint (at the wrist). These two joints work as a mechanical pair — together they create the approximately 150° of forearm rotation that positions the hand for all manual activities (typing, turning a key, pouring from a cup). Restrictions in either joint reduce functional forearm rotation.

Elbow circumduction comprehensively mobilises both radioulnar joints through their rotational range, in addition to cycling the humeroulnar joint through its flexion-extension range. This combined mobilisation is more complete than elbow bending alone.

Anterior and posterior elbow compartment mobilisation

The circumduction motion stretches the anterior elbow capsule (during extension arc) and the posterior capsule (during flexion arc) — the capsular structures that most commonly restrict elbow extension and full flexion respectively in arthritis.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — three-dimensional elbow mobilisation; radioulnar joint conditioning; forearm pronator-supinator reactivation.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Elbow (A003) Multi-planar elbow mobilisation; full capsular stretching
Forearm Pronation/Supination Restriction Radioulnar joint mobilisation — specific to this exercise
Tennis Elbow / Golfer's Elbow Forearm rotation conditioning; tendon gliding
All IAYT protocols (preparatory) Complete elbow group preparation; transitions to shoulder group

6. Contraindications

  • Acute elbow injury — reduce range, avoid forced rotation
  • Radial head fracture (acute) — radioulnar rotation loads the fracture site; immobilise until healed

7. Variations & Progressions

Elbow circumduction (standard): As described.

Combining with wrist rotation: Simultaneously rotate wrists while performing elbow circles — mobilises the entire forearm-wrist chain.

Sequencing context: Final exercise in the hand/wrist/elbow group. Transitions to the shoulder group beginning with P0020.
