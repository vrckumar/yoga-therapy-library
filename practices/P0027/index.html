Neck Movement and Rotation

Neck Movement and Rotation (Greeva Sanchalana — Bhramana)

A combined cervical sequence that integrates linear cervical movements with full circumduction rotation. Similar to P0026 but with greater emphasis on the rotational component — typically used as the complete cervical warm-up for conditions where rotational mobility (atlantoaxial joint, deep cervical rotators) is the primary concern.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Seated (Dandasana) or Tadasana.

Practice:

  1. Begin with 3 rounds of each directional movement: forward-backward, right-left rotation, right-left lateral bending.
  2. Then perform 10 slow, full cervical circumduction circles clockwise.
  3. Reverse: 10 anti-clockwise.
  4. Complete with 3 rounds of each directional movement again (same as step 1) — to return all planes to the linear movement pattern after the circular work.

Breathing: Normal throughout circumduction. Exhale on each directional movement.

2. Anatomical Activation

Same as P0026, with additional emphasis from the extended circumduction:

  • Atlantoaxial joint: specifically mobilised in the rotational apex of each circumduction circle
  • Deep cervical rotators (rotatores cervicis): the deepest segmental rotators of the cervical spine — activated throughout circumduction

3. Physiological Effects

Extended circumduction — atlantoaxial emphasis

The extended circumduction sequence (10 circles vs 3–5 in P0026) specifically targets the atlantoaxial joint, which provides approximately 50% of total cervical rotation. Prolonged, slow circumduction maintains the rotational mobility of this critical joint.

Deep cervical rotator conditioning

The rotatores cervicis — the deepest segmental muscles of the cervical spine — are the primary proprioceptive sensors of the cervical spine and the primary segmental stabilisers at each cervical level. These muscles are inhibited by pain and chronic poor posture. The slow, coordinated circumduction movements reactivate these deep stabilisers.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — atlantoaxial and full cervical 3D mobilisation; deep cervical rotator reactivation; proprioceptive retraining.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Cervical Rotational Stiffness Extended circumduction specifically targets rotational restriction
Arthritis — Cervical Spine (A003) Atlantoaxial joint mobilisation — the primary rotation joint
Cervicogenic Headache Deep cervical rotator reactivation
All IAYT protocols More extensive cervical warm-up when time and clinical indication support it

6. Contraindications

Same as P0026; circumduction contraindicated in vertebral artery insufficiency, myelopathy, and atlantoaxial instability.

7. Variations & Progressions

Reduced circumduction (modified): For spondylotic or elderly patients — 5 circles each direction only; smaller amplitude.

Sequencing context: More extensive version of P0026. Used when cervical rotation is specifically targeted. Progresses to P0028 (Side Movement of Neck) and P0029 (Neck Press and Release).
