Sastanga Namaskara

Sastanga Namaskara (Eight-Limbed Salutation)

Sasta = eight, anga = limbs, namaskara = salutation/prostration. The eight-limbed prostration — a position in which exactly eight body parts contact the floor simultaneously: the two feet (toes), two knees, the chest (or abdomen), the chin, and the two palms. This is Position 5 (or 6 in the 12-step version) in the Surya Namaskar sequence, interposed between Parvatasana (inverted V) and Bhujangasana (cobra). Unlike a full push-up bottom position, the buttocks remain raised, creating a distinctive undulating spine with the middle of the body in contact with the floor but the hips elevated.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Within the Surya Namaskar flow — arrived at from the plank position (Step 4) with the body inclined at approximately 30° to the floor.

Practice:

  1. From the plank position (body straight, hands and toes on the floor), inhale and glide the body forward without moving the hands or feet.
  2. Exhaling: rest the forehead or chin, chest, palms, knees, and toes on the floor simultaneously. The buttocks remain raised — they do not touch the floor. The elbows bend and press into the sides of the body.
  3. Eight points of the body contact the floor: (1) chin, (2) chest, (3) right palm, (4) left palm, (5) right knee, (6) left knee, (7) right toes, (8) left toes. Hence the name Sastanga (eight-limbed) Namaskara.
  4. Hold briefly, then proceed to Step 6 (Bhujangasana / cobra) — inhale and raise the trunk into the cobra.

Breathing: Exhale as the body lowers to the eight-point contact; inhale as you rise into Bhujangasana.

Duration: Brief hold (1–2 seconds) as a transition position within Surya Namaskar.

2. Anatomical Activation

Body parts and muscles

  • Chin on floor: cervical flexors release; cervical extensors (briefly eccentric); the trachea and pharynx are in a mild compression
  • Chest on floor: pectoralis major and minor are compressed; anterior intercostals are in contact with the floor
  • Palms on floor: wrist extensors loaded; triceps active to control the lowering
  • Knees on floor: quadriceps eccentrically control the knee-contact; knee joint is in mild flexion
  • Toes on floor: plantarflexors (gastrocnemius, soleus) and intrinsic foot muscles engaged

The "raised buttocks" element

The most distinctive feature of Sastanga Namaskara is the elevated buttocks while the chest is on the floor. This creates:

  • A marked lumbar and thoracic extension (spine bows upward to accommodate the hips-up, chest-down geometry)
  • Active gluteus maximus and hamstring contraction to hold the hips elevated
  • Pre-stretching of the anterior hip and psoas before the subsequent Bhujangasana

3. Physiological Effects

Acupressure and pressure-point activation

The systematic contact of eight specific body regions with the floor in Sastanga Namaskara applies pressure to:

  • The Manipura (navel) and Anahata (heart/chest) chakra regions via the floor contact
  • The knee acupressure points (Stomach meridian points on the anterior knee)
  • The palmar pressure points (Pericardium meridian in the palm)

In the yogic tradition, this simultaneous stimulation of multiple pressure points during the transitional moment of the salutation is considered the purpose of this specific position.

Transitional spinal preparation

Sastanga Namaskara serves a biomechanical preparation function — by bringing the sternum and chin to the floor with hips raised, it pre-positions the thoracic spine in a mild extension curve from which the subsequent Bhujangasana (cobra) can be entered smoothly. The palms remain at the sides of the chest throughout, already in the cobra hand position.

Chest and anterior thorax stimulation

The chest contact with the floor applies a mild external compression to the anterior rib cage and sternum. On rising into cobra, this compression is released — a mechanical respiratory activation that stimulates the anterior lung lobes.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — prostration posture; brief spinal extension preparation.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the position corresponds to a moment of complete breath surrender (end-exhale position); associated with apana vayu at the lowest point of the Surya Namaskar arc.

7. Contraindications

  • Pregnancy: prone positioning with direct abdominal contact not appropriate after first trimester
  • Recent abdominal surgery: avoid direct anterior abdominal pressure
  • Knee injury: the knee-contact with the floor may be painful; use knee padding

8. Note on Name Variants

Sastanga Namaskara is also rendered as Sashtanga Namaskara, Ashtanga Namaskara, or Ashtanga Dandavat. All refer to the same eight-limbed floor prostration. Not to be confused with the Ashtanga Yoga system, which takes its name from the eight-limbed path of Patanjali rather than this posture.
