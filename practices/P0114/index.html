Shithilikarana Vyayama

Shithilikarana Vyayama (Sithilikarana Vyayama — Complete Loosening Exercise System)

Sithila (Sanskrit) = loose, relaxed; karana = making, causing; vyayama = exercise, practice. The Loosening Exercise System — the entire corpus of preparatory joint-mobilisation and warming exercises that form the first module of every IAYT yoga therapy session at SVYASA. This entry is the comprehensive parent page describing the system as a whole; individual component exercises are documented in P0001–P0056.

1. What Shithilikarana Vyayama Is

Shithilikarana Vyayama is not a single pose but a system of exercises — the foundational warming and mobilisation practices of the SVYASA IAYT tradition. The system encompasses:

  1. Passive joint mobilisation exercises (instructor-assisted or self-assisted movement of individual joint elements)
  2. Active joint mobilisation exercises (the practitioner actively moves each joint through its full range)
  3. Dynamic spinal exercises (forward-backward bending, side bending, twisting, swinging)
  4. Vigorous whole-body exercises (jogging, jumping, swimming movements, climbing, walking variations)

The system progresses from the most distal, smallest joints (toes) through the ankle, knee, hip, wrist, elbow, shoulder, and neck — ending with integrated spinal and whole-body movements.

3. Structural Principles of the System

Progression principle

The exercises move from periphery to centre, from simple to complex, and from passive to active:

  • Periphery first (toes, fingers, ankles, wrists) — prepares the circulation and neural pathways before loading the weight-bearing joints
  • Simple before complex — single-joint movements precede multi-joint dynamic movements
  • Passive before active — in the Rheumatology protocol especially, passive exercises precede active to avoid overloading inflamed joints

Sthiti (position) discipline

Each phase of the Shithilikarana Vyayama is performed from a defined starting position (Sthiti) and returns to a defined relaxation position (Sithila):

  • Standing: Tadasana → Sithila Tadasana
  • Seated: Dandasana → Sithila Dandasana
  • Prone: Prone Sthiti → Makarasana
  • Supine: Supine Sthiti → Savasana

This structural framework prevents the common mistakes of rushing between exercises and neglecting the integrative relaxation moments between them.

Breath awareness from the start

Even in the simplest toe-rotation exercise, the Shithilikarana Vyayama system emphasises breath awareness throughout. This is not merely incidental — the development of breath awareness during easy movement is a preparatory step for the more demanding breath-movement coordination of pranayama.

4. Therapeutic Applications Across IAYT Protocols

Protocol Emphasis in Shithilikarana
Arthritis (A003) Full passive + Sakti Vikasaka series; gradual progression; avoid forcing inflamed joints
Back Pain (A004) Lumbar stretch exercises; neck mobilisation; spinal flexion-extension series
Diabetes (A001) Vigorous dynamic phase; walking variations; abdominal exercises
Obesity (A002) Extended vigorous phase; stepping, jogging, jumping, frog jumps
Asthma (A007) Moderate; avoid hyperventilation-type exercises; emphasise Sasankasana breathing
Hypertension (A005) Gentle pace; avoid prolonged inverted positions; relaxation emphasis
Anxiety/Depression (A006) Full sequence at moderate pace; neck series with mantra for Manomaya Kosha
GID (A014) Emphasis on abdominal exercises; full butterfly; cycling; Pavanamuktasana kriya

5. Duration and Format

Full comprehensive session: 30–45 minutes (all groups performed completely).

Standard session opener: 15–20 minutes (joint mobilisation + selected dynamic exercises appropriate to the protocol).

Abbreviated session: 5–10 minutes (neck series + shoulder + basic spinal mobility) — used when time is limited.
