Chair Ardha Chakrasana and Padahastasana (Backward and Forward Bend in Chair)

The standing backward bend (Ardha Chakrasana) and forward bend (Padahastasana) adapted for chair yoga, performed in standing Tadasana adjacent to the chair. The patient alternates between backward bending (Ardha Chakrasana) and forward bending (Padahastasana), holding onto or standing near the chair for balance support. Used as Step 7 of the Yoga-Chair-Breathing protocol for asthma. Trains the full anterior-posterior spinal movement arc, expands the thoracic cage (on the backward bend), and releases the posterior chain (on the forward bend) — all with the safety of the chair nearby.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana — stand erect beside or behind the chair. The chair can be used for balance support as needed.

Form A — Movements only (5 times):

  1. From Tadasana, slowly move to Ardha Chakrasana — hands rising overhead and bending backward gently from the waist.
  2. Return to Tadasana.
  3. Continue into Padahastasana — bend forward from the waist, allowing the hands to hang loosely down toward the floor.
  4. Return to Tadasana.
  5. This backward-to-forward arc is one cycle. Repeat 5 times continuously.

Form B — Movements with breathing (5 times):

  1. Inhaling: move to Ardha Chakrasana (backward bend — chest opens to inhalation).
  2. Exhaling: move to Padahastasana (forward bend — chest compresses on exhalation).
  3. Synchronise precisely. Repeat 5 times.

Form C — Movements with M-kara (5 times):

  1. Chant "M-kara" (the MMM humming sound) in a low voice while bending forward into Padahastasana.
  2. The M-kara exhalation deepens the forward bend and produces thoracic vibration.
  3. Repeat 5 times.

Notes:

  • In Padahastasana, the hands hang loosely — do not force the hands to reach the floor if it is not comfortable.
  • Use the chair back for support in the backward bend if balance is uncertain.
  • Move smoothly and continuously — no abrupt stops.

2. Anatomical Activation

Ardha Chakrasana phase (backward bend, inhalation):

  • Erector spinae: concentrically active as the spine extends
  • Rectus abdominis and hip flexors: eccentrically stretched
  • Anterior intercostals: stretched as the thoracic cage opens
  • Shoulder flexors (deltoid, biceps): active in the arm-raise phase

Padahastasana phase (forward bend, exhalation):

  • Erector spinae: eccentrically lengthened
  • Hamstrings: stretched in the hip-flexed forward fold
  • Gastrocnemius and soleus: secondary posterior chain stretch
  • Thoracic extensors: passively stretched in the folded-over position

3. Physiological Effects

Thoracic expansion and bronchial opening (asthma protocol)

The Ardha Chakrasana phase is specifically valuable in the Yoga-Chair-Breathing protocol because:

  • The backward bend with arm elevation produces maximum thoracic expansion
  • The anterior chest and ribs are stretched — directly opening the compressed, constricted thoracic cage of the asthmatic patient
  • The inhalation-backward-bend coupling exploits the natural respiratory-locomotor coupling for maximum lung expansion

Forward bend parasympathetic activation

The Padahastasana exhalation phase:

  • Compresses the thorax on exhalation
  • Facilitates complete exhalation — critical in asthma, where residual volume is increased and complete exhalation is difficult
  • Activates parasympathetic tone through the forward-fold mechanism (same as Sasankasana)

Spinal mobility — continuous arc

The continuous alternation between full spinal extension and full flexion (10 cycles in the combined Form A+B practice) produces the same comprehensive spinal mobilisation as the Alternate Bhujangasana and Parvatasana (P0148), but in the standing position — more accessible for patients who cannot perform prone exercises.

M-kara vibration (Step 7 of YCB)

The M-kara humming on the Padahastasana exhalation:

  • Creates low-frequency thoracic vibration that helps mobilise bronchial secretions
  • Stimulates the vagal bronchial branches through mechanical vibration
  • Slows the exhalation (the hum must sustain for several seconds), training prolonged exhalation — the key respiratory retraining target in asthma

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — thoracic expansion and posterior chain stretching; spinal extension and flexion arc.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the inhalation-extension coupling activates prana vayu at the anterior chest; the exhalation-forward-fold deepens apana vayu in the lower body.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Asthma (A007) Primary — Step 7 of Yoga-Chair-Breathing; thoracic expansion, M-kara vibration, exhalation training
Back Pain (A004) Standing spinal mobilisation with chair support
Elderly / balance-impaired patients Chair provides safety for standing spinal movements
Obesity (A002) Standing dynamic spinal movement
Anxiety (A006) Rhythmic backward-forward movement with breath produces calming

6. Contraindications

  • Severe lumbar disc prolapse: deep forward bending (Padahastasana) may aggravate; limit the forward range
  • Severe hypertension: vigorous bending may temporarily affect blood pressure; perform slowly
  • Glaucoma: forward-fold head-below-heart position; assess appropriateness
  • Balance impairment: ensure the chair is secure and within reach at all times
