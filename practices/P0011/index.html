Knee Bending and Rotation

Knee Bending and Rotation (Janu Naman Chakra)

The combined knee sequence integrating full flexion-extension (P0009) with tibial rotation (P0010). Used as the standard comprehensive knee preparation practice in IAYT protocols, providing both sagittal-plane and rotational mobilisation in one continuous sequence.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Standing (Tadasana) with support, or Seated.

Practice:

  1. Begin with 5 rounds of Knee Bending (P0009) — full flexion and extension on right leg.
  2. At mid-flexion (approximately 90°), perform 5 clockwise and 5 anti-clockwise small tibial rotations (P0010).
  3. Complete with 5 more rounds of full knee bending to return to the sagittal axis.
  4. Repeat on left leg.

Breathing: Exhale on flexion; inhale on extension; normal during rotation.

2. Anatomical Activation

Combines all structures from P0009 and P0010:

  • Hamstrings, quadriceps, gastrocnemius (sagittal plane)
  • Popliteus, biceps femoris short head (rotation)
  • Medial and lateral menisci (mobilised in both planes)
  • MCL, LCL, posterior capsule

3. Physiological Effects

The combined practice provides comprehensive three-dimensional knee joint mobilisation:

  • Full articular surface coverage: sagittal bending distributes synovial fluid across the anterior-posterior axis; rotation distributes fluid to medial-lateral recesses
  • Complete meniscal mobilisation: translation (bending) and rotation address all components of meniscal movement
  • Coordinated multi-plane motor control: the transition between bending and rotation trains smooth coordination of all knee muscle groups — the most functional movement pattern for activities like rising from a chair or walking on uneven ground

The combined form represents normal knee kinematics: the knee during gait combines flexion-extension with axial tibial rotation at each step (the "screw-home" mechanism at terminal extension). Practising this combined movement restores the natural kinematic pattern.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — three-dimensional knee mobilisation; normal kinematic pattern restoration; comprehensive synovial fluid distribution.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Knee (A003) Most complete knee mobilisation — all compartments and planes
All IAYT protocols (preparatory) Standard combined knee exercise when full individual sequence is not used
Post-knee surgery Three-dimensional ROM restoration — standard rehabilitation goal
Back Pain (A004) Restores normal knee kinematics, which directly affects lumbar-pelvic mechanics

6. Contraindications

Same as P0009 and P0010 combined. Acute ligamentous or meniscal injury — avoid. Acute effusion — reduce range.

7. Variations & Progressions

Shortened protocol (standard): 5 bends + 5 rotations each direction + 5 bends per leg.

Sequencing context: Follows P0009–P0010. Progresses to P0012 (Vertical Stretch/Straight Leg Raise).
