Chair Neck Movement

Chair Neck Movement (Seated Neck Exercises — Chair Adaptation)

The complete neck movement series (corresponding to P0023–P0029 in the Sithilikarana Vyayama) adapted for patients who cannot sit on the floor. All neck exercises — forward-backward flexion, lateral flexion, rotation, and combined movements — are performed while seated upright in a chair. The chair adaptation makes the neck series accessible to elderly patients, those with lower limb conditions, those recovering from surgery, or anyone for whom floor-seated postures are not appropriate. Clinical benefit is equivalent to the floor-seated versions.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Seated upright in a sturdy chair. Feet flat on the floor. Spine erect. Hands resting on the thighs or armrests. Eyes closed.

The full neck series in chair:

1. Forward-backward bending (5 rounds):

  • Gently drop the chin toward the chest (flexion). Feel the stretch in the posterior neck.
  • Return to neutral. Gently drop the head backward (extension) — only to a comfortable range.
  • Inhale as the head goes back; exhale as the chin drops forward.

2. Lateral bending (5 rounds each side):

  • Drop the right ear toward the right shoulder — do not raise the shoulder.
  • Return to centre. Drop the left ear toward the left shoulder.
  • Feel the stretch on the opposite side.

3. Rotation (5 rounds each side):

  • Slowly turn the head to the right, looking over the right shoulder.
  • Return to centre. Turn to the left.

4. Combined (half-circle, avoiding full extension in cervical spondylosis):

  • Drop the chin to the chest, then slowly roll the head toward the right ear, pause, then return through the centre to the left ear position. Return to centre.
  • (Omit the backward extension arc in cervical spondylosis.)

5. Neck movements with breathing (5 rounds):

  • Inhale as the head tilts back (extension); exhale as the chin drops forward.

6. Neck movements with mantra (5 rounds):

  • Chant "A-kara" (AH sound) softly while bending the neck forward.
  • This adds the resonance of sound vibration to the neck movement.

Notes:

  • Move slowly and gently; no jerking.
  • The chair provides additional stability that may be especially beneficial for patients with balance concerns.
  • Keep the shoulders fully relaxed and lowered throughout.

2. Anatomical Activation

Identical to floor-seated neck exercises (P0023–P0029):

  • Cervical flexors (SCM, longus colli): forward flexion exercises
  • Cervical extensors (splenius, semispinalis, deep suboccipitals): backward extension exercises
  • Lateral flexors (scalenes, SCM, upper trapezius): lateral bending exercises
  • Cervical rotators (SCM, splenius, scalenes): rotation exercises

3. Physiological Effects

Same as the floor-seated neck series but with the added benefits of:

  • Postural support: the chair back provides mild lumbar support, allowing patients with lumbar pain to focus on the cervical movements without lumbar discomfort
  • Fall safety: patients with balance impairment can perform neck exercises safely (head movements can cause brief dizziness; the chair prevents falling)
  • Psychological accessibility: many patients — particularly elderly or hospital-based — are more comfortable with chair-based exercises and demonstrate better adherence

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — cervical mobilisation; neck muscle flexibility and strength.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — cervical mobility supports pranic circulation between head and trunk.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Elderly patients (all conditions) Chair support ensures safety
Lower limb conditions preventing floor-sitting Knee, hip, or ankle restrictions overcome
Hospital/bedside settings Chair available when floor is not
Post-surgical patients Safe seated position during recovery
Cervical Spondylosis (A004) Modified range with chair support
All IAYT protocols (elderly/adapted version) Universal accessibility adaptation

6. Contraindications

Same as floor-seated neck exercises plus:

  • Cervical spondylosis with significant cord compression: extension movements limited or omitted
  • Severe vestibular disorders: perform at reduced speed; discontinue if vertigo is triggered

7. Relationship to Floor-Seated Version

This is the chair adaptation of P0023–P0029 (Sithilikarana Vyayama neck series). All technique and benefit details are equivalent — the only difference is the seated support platform. See also Chair Neck Movement in Vajrasana (P0133) for the variation in which the chair is used as support while sitting in a Vajrasana-like position.
