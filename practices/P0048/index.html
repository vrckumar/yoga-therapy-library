Hip Stretch

Hip Stretch (Shroni Akunchana Prasarana)

Supine hip external rotation and flexion stretch — a supine variation of hip opening that brings the ankle of one leg onto the opposite knee (figure-4 / piriformis stretch position) and applies pressure to deepen the hip stretch. A targeted piriformis and posterior hip capsule mobilisation.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Supine Posture. Knees bent, feet flat on floor.

Practice:

  1. Lie on the back with both knees bent, feet flat.
  2. Place the right ankle on the left knee (figure-4 position) — right hip is in external rotation and abduction.
  3. Flex the left hip by lifting the left foot off the floor or grasping the back of the left thigh.
  4. Draw the left knee toward the chest — this deepens the stretch in the right posterior hip and piriformis.
  5. Hold for 5–10 breaths. Maintain normal breathing.
  6. Release. Repeat on the left side.

Breathing: Normal throughout; exhale as the knee draws closer.

Duration: 5–10 breaths each side.

2. Anatomical Activation

Primary structures stretched

  • Piriformis: the primary target — the figure-4 position with hip flexion elongates the piriformis maximally
  • External hip rotators (obturator internus/externus, gemelli, quadratus femoris): all elongated in the externally rotated, flexed hip position
  • Posterior hip capsule (iliofemoral ligament, ischiofemoral ligament): stretched in the flexed, externally rotated hip position
  • Gluteus maximus and medius (posterior fibres): elongated

3. Physiological Effects

Piriformis elongation — the defining therapeutic effect

The figure-4 + hip flexion position provides the maximum elongation of the piriformis muscle available in a non-invasive, body-weight-only stretch. This position is the standard physiotherapy treatment for piriformis syndrome and is recommended as both prevention and treatment for sciatica associated with piriformis compression.

The piriformis elongation in this position (combined hip flexion + internal rotation of the fixed leg being drawn to chest + external rotation of the crossed leg) is significantly greater than any other self-stretching position for the piriformis.

Posterior hip capsule decompression

The posterior hip capsule is frequently tightened in hip osteoarthritis and in individuals who sit for prolonged periods. The figure-4 stretch places the posterior capsule in maximum elongation, restoring the posterior capsule compliance that allows full internal rotation and extension at the hip.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — piriformis and posterior hip capsule elongation; sciatic nerve decompression; posterior hip mobility restoration.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Piriformis Syndrome Direct piriformis elongation — primary treatment
Sciatica (piriformis component) Decompression of sciatic nerve at piriformis; neural mobilisation
Arthritis — Hip (A003) Posterior capsule mobilisation; external rotator flexibility
Back Pain (A004) Hip external rotator flexibility reduces SI joint and lumbar compensation
All IAYT protocols Targeted hip stretch in the preparatory sequence

6. Contraindications

  • Hip replacement (posterior approach) — the figure-4 + hip flexion position is the primary dislocation risk position for posterior-approach hip replacements; contraindicated
  • Acute piriformis syndrome — gentle range only; avoid forceful end-range loading
  • Acute sciatica (severe) — neural tension in end-range stretch may aggravate

7. Variations & Progressions

Without drawing leg to chest (modified): Stay in figure-4 with foot on floor — less piriformis elongation; suitable for very tight or acute presentations.

Thread the needle: From the figure-4, thread the right arm through the gap between the legs and interlace fingers behind the left thigh — maximum stretch with arm assistance.

Sequencing context: Follows P0047 (Full Butterfly). Part of the hip opening subgroup before the lumbar stretching series (P0049–P0056).
