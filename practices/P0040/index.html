Cycling (Chakra Padasanchalana)

Supine leg cycling — a variant of P0039 (Padasanchalana). Where P0039 may emphasise the alternating single-leg cycle, this form typically involves both legs cycling simultaneously in a double-leg pedalling pattern, or emphasises a larger, more vigorous cycling arc. In IAYT protocols, P0039 and P0040 may be used interchangeably or sequentially as a combined cycling series.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Supine Posture.

Practice — Double-leg cycling:

  1. Lie flat. Raise both legs off the floor simultaneously.
  2. Pedal both legs in a coordinated cycling movement — one leg extends as the other bends — but both remain elevated above the floor throughout.
  3. The legs do not touch the floor during the practice.
  4. Perform 20 cycles forward; 20 cycles backward.

Practice — Large arc cycling: The legs may be cycled through a larger arc — extending further from the body and bringing the knees closer to the chest — for greater hip ROM mobilisation.

Breathing: Normal; some coordinate with the movement.

Duration: 20 cycles each direction.

2. Anatomical Activation

Similar to P0039 but with:

  • Greater hip flexor and abdominal load: both legs elevated throughout — constant hip flexor and abdominal isometric demand (no rest phase when a leg touches the floor)
  • Rectus femoris: maximally engaged maintaining extension while elevated
  • Transversus abdominis and rectus abdominis: increased demand compared to P0039 due to both legs being simultaneously elevated

3. Physiological Effects

Increased core stabilisation demand

With both legs elevated throughout (not touching the floor), the lumbar spine must be continuously stabilised against the bilateral hip flexor pull. This represents a greater abdominal demand than P0039 and constitutes a core stabilisation training stimulus.

Continuous joint loading

The absence of a floor contact phase means the hip and knee joints are continuously loaded through the range of motion — a continuous synovial fluid distribution stimulus without rest intervals.

For all other physiological mechanisms, see P0039 — the mechanisms are identical but the intensity is higher.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — higher-intensity hip-knee cycling; abdominal core strengthening; lower limb dynamic mobilisation.

5. Indicated For

Same as P0039 but higher intensity — suitable for those who have progressed beyond the basic P0039 level.

Note: For acute back pain, P0039 (alternating legs) is preferred over P0040 (both legs elevated) — the bilateral elevation creates greater intradiscal pressure.

6. Contraindications

  • Lumbar disc herniation (any acute phase) — bilateral elevated leg position significantly increases intradiscal pressure; use P0039 (alternating) or avoid
  • Core weakness (very deconditioned) — lumbar hyperextension risk; begin with P0039

7. Variations & Progressions

P0039 → P0040 progression: Begin with alternating feet-touching-floor (P0039), progress to both legs elevated (P0040) as core strength allows.

Sequencing context: Follows P0039. Progresses to P0041 (Sit-up).
