Back Stretch with Alternate Leg

Back Stretch with Alternate Leg (Ekapadasana Prishtha Naman)

Supine single knee-to-chest stretch — one knee drawn toward the chest while the other leg remains extended. The targeted unilateral lumbar and hip flexor stretch, and the gentlest entry point in the lumbar stretching series.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Supine Posture.

Practice:

  1. Lie flat. Keep the left leg extended.
  2. While exhaling, bend the right knee and draw it toward the chest — hold below the knee (or on the shin) with both hands.
  3. Pull the right knee firmly toward the chest. The right hip flexes; the right thigh compresses the right lower abdomen.
  4. Lift the head and bring the chin toward the right knee (optional — if comfortable).
  5. Hold for 5 breaths with normal breathing.
  6. Release. Extend the right leg.
  7. Repeat on the left.
  8. Perform 5 rounds alternating.

Breathing: Exhale on drawing the knee; breathe normally during the hold; inhale on release.

2. Anatomical Activation

Structures stretched/mobilised

  • Right hip flexors (iliopsoas, rectus femoris) of the extended left leg: the single knee-to-chest position places the contralateral hip flexors in maximum elongation — the extended left leg stretches the left iliopsoas as the right hip flexes
  • Lumbar extensors (erector spinae, multifidus): elongated as the spine flattens against the floor
  • Sacroiliac joint: the unilateral knee-to-chest applies a specific mobilising force to the ipsilateral SI joint
  • Piriformis: mild elongation in the hip flexion position

3. Physiological Effects

Iliopsoas stretch (contralateral)

When one knee is drawn to the chest, the opposite hip is placed in extension relative to the pelvis. This elongates the contralateral iliopsoas — the hip flexor of the extended leg. This is a specific stretch for the iliopsoas that is difficult to achieve in other supine positions.

Unilateral sacroiliac joint mobilisation

The single knee-to-chest applies a flexion-distraction force to the ipsilateral sacroiliac joint — the same manoeuvre used by osteopaths to mobilise a restricted SI joint (the "knee chest technique"). This makes it the most targeted SI joint mobilisation in the supine series.

Colon compression and gas release

The right knee drawn to the chest compresses the ascending colon (right lower abdomen); the left knee to chest compresses the descending and sigmoid colon. This is the mechanism of the Pavanamuktasana (P0090) gas release, applied here in a milder, preparatory form.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — contralateral iliopsoas elongation; sacroiliac joint mobilisation; lumbar extensor elongation; colonic compression.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Back Pain (A004) Lumbar extensor elongation; SI joint mobilisation; iliopsoas stretching
Sacroiliac Joint Dysfunction Direct SI joint mobilisation technique
Hip Flexor Tightness Contralateral iliopsoas elongation
GID / Constipation Colonic compression — preparatory Pavanamuktasana variation
All IAYT protocols Entry-level lumbar stretching; preparation for full lumbar stretches (P0051–P0056)

6. Contraindications

  • Acute lumbar disc herniation — hip flexion on the affected side may aggravate; use very gentle range
  • Acute hip bursitis (trochanteric) — hip flexion may load the inflamed bursa
  • Recent abdominal surgery — knee-to-chest abdominal compression; avoid until healed

7. Variations & Progressions

Without head lift: Omit the chin-to-knee for cervical spine protection.

Bilateral (P0051): Both knees to chest — lumbar decompression practice.

Sequencing context: Begins the lumbar stretching subgroup within the supine leg series. Progresses to P0050–P0056.
