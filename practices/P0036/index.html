Jogging

Jogging (Sthana Dhavana)

On-the-spot jogging — the cardiovascular warm-up component of the Sithilikarana Vyayama series. The YIC Manual 2 describes a multi-stage progressive jogging sequence (slow, backward heel-to-buttock, forward high-knees, sideways) that systematically raises heart rate, circulates lymph, and prepares the entire lower limb musculature before posture practice.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana. Make loose fists and hold them on the chest throughout.

Practice — 4 Stages (from YIC Manual 2):

Stage I — Slow Jogging:

  1. Start jogging on the toes slowly. 20 times.
  2. Gradually increase to 100 times with practice.

Stage II — Backward Jogging (heel to buttock):

  1. Lean slightly forward; increase speed.
  2. Hit the buttocks with the heels. Repeat 20 times at maximum comfortable speed.
  3. Gradually slow down. Continue slow jogging for 10 rounds before moving to next stage.

Stage III — Forward Jogging (high knees):

  1. Lean slightly backward; increase speed, raising the knees toward the chest.
  2. Repeat 20 times at maximum speed.
  3. Slow down to gentle jogging for 10 rounds.

Stage IV — Side Jogging:

  1. Increase speed; take the heels sideways bringing them toward the elbows.
  2. Repeat 20 times. Slow down.

Stage V — Mukha Dhauti (post-jogging breathing): Lean forward, palms on thighs; inhale through nose, exhale forcibly through mouth.

Duration: The full 4-stage sequence takes approximately 5–8 minutes.

2. Anatomical Activation

  • Gastrocnemius and soleus: primary running muscles; calf-pump activity maximal
  • Quadriceps and hamstrings: alternating activation through the running gait cycle
  • Hip flexors (iliopsoas): maximally activated in Stage III (high-knee jogging)
  • Gluteus maximus: maximally activated in Stage II (backward heel-to-buttock — hip extension)
  • Hip abductors: Stage IV (sideways jogging)
  • Cardiovascular system: heart rate elevation to 50–70% of maximum in Stage II–III

3. Physiological Effects

Cardiovascular warm-up — the primary function

Jogging is the most efficient cardiovascular warm-up in the Sithilikarana series. It raises heart rate, increases cardiac output, dilates peripheral blood vessels, and elevates core body temperature — all prerequisites for safe, effective asana practice. The multi-stage format systematically raises exercise intensity and prevents sudden high-intensity work.

Lymphatic circulation

The lymphatic system has no pump and depends entirely on skeletal muscle contraction for lymph propulsion. The rhythmic contraction of the lower limb muscles during jogging is among the most efficient lymphatic circulation stimuli available — explaining the subjective sensation of lightness and reduced lower limb oedema after jogging practice.

Bone density stimulation — ground reaction forces

The impact loading of jogging generates ground reaction forces of approximately 2–3× body weight at each footstrike. These forces are transmitted through the skeletal system and stimulate osteoblastic activity according to Wolff's Law. Regular jogging practice contributes to bone density maintenance, particularly in the femoral neck and calcaneus — primary osteoporotic fracture sites.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — cardiovascular activation; lower limb muscle warming; lymphatic circulation; bone loading.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — jogging generates the heat of tapas; the rhythmic breath of jogging activates and equalises the flow of prana vayu through the body.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Obesity (A002) Caloric expenditure; cardiovascular fitness improvement; metabolic rate elevation
Diabetes (A001) Glucose utilisation improvement; insulin sensitivity enhancement
Hypertension (A006) Aerobic conditioning improves vascular compliance and reduces resting blood pressure
Depression / Anxiety Aerobic exercise is among the most evidence-based interventions for mood elevation
Osteoporosis Impact loading stimulates bone formation (Wolff's Law)

6. Contraindications

  • Acute knee or ankle injury — impact loading on healing structures; use Stage I (slow) only or substitute with walking
  • Severe cardiac arrhythmia — elevated heart rate may be unsafe; use very gentle Stage I only; medical clearance
  • Severe osteoporosis — impact loading risk of vertebral or femoral fracture; substitute with walking

7. Variations & Progressions

Full 4-stage (standard): As described in YIC Manual 2.

Stage I only (beginner/modified): Slow jogging only — minimal cardiovascular demand, safe for all.

Sequencing context: First in the aerobic/dynamic group. Typically performed before or after the joint mobilisation exercises to warm up the cardiovascular system. Progresses to P0037 (Jumping).
