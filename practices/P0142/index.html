Bhujabanda Sakti Vikasaka

Bhujabanda Sakti Vikasaka (Shoulder Strengthening)

Bhuja = arm/shoulder, banda = bond/armband, sakti = strength, vikasaka = developer. The shoulder-strengthening exercise of the Sakti Vikasaka series. Both forearms are held parallel to the ground (at 90° to the upper arms) with clenched fists facing each other, and then both arms are forcefully pushed straight forward to shoulder level and pulled back to the starting position. This dynamic push-pull isolates the shoulder anterior deltoid (push) and posterior deltoid/rhomboids (pull) — the primary stabilisers of glenohumeral function.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana.

Practice:

  1. Make fists of both hands with the thumbs tucked inside.
  2. Bend the elbows and raise the forearms so they are parallel to the ground, fists facing each other (the forearms form a 90° angle with the upper arms). The fists are at approximately chest height, knuckles facing inward.
  3. Now push both arms forward forcefully and vigorously to full extension at shoulder level. The arms should be parallel to the ground when fully extended, with palms facing upward.
  4. Pull the arms back to the starting position (forearms parallel to ground, fists at chest).
  5. When pulled back, the elbows must not go behind the body (elbows stay at the body line).
  6. Repeat 20 times.

Notes:

  • When pushed forward, the arms are parallel to the ground and palms face upward.
  • The movement is forceful and vigorous, not slow and controlled.
  • Normal breathing throughout.

2. Anatomical Activation

Primary muscles

  • Anterior deltoid and pectoralis major (clavicular head): concentrically active in the forward push
  • Triceps brachii: locks the elbow in extension at the end of the push phase
  • Posterior deltoid, teres major, latissimus dorsi: concentrically active in the pull-back phase
  • Rhomboids and middle trapezius: active in scapular retraction during the return phase
  • Rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, subscapularis): stabilise the glenohumeral joint throughout both phases

Shoulder rehabilitation specifics

This is a horizontal push-pull exercise at shoulder level — the most fundamental plane of shoulder function for daily activities (pushing doors, lifting, reaching). The 90° starting position (forearms parallel to ground) is the mid-range of shoulder function — accessible even to patients with reduced shoulder range of motion.

3. Physiological Effects

Glenohumeral joint strengthening

Rheumatoid arthritis frequently affects the shoulder joint (glenohumeral, acromioclavicular), where the synovial membrane is extensive. Progressive shoulder strengthening:

  • Maintains the rotator cuff activation needed to keep the humeral head centred in the glenoid
  • Reduces the superior migration of the humeral head associated with rotator cuff weakness
  • Preserves the functional pushing and pulling strength needed for independence in ADLs

Scapulothoracic rhythm restoration

The push-pull pattern trains the serratus anterior (push phase) and rhomboids (pull phase) — the two muscles most critical for normal scapulothoracic rhythm. Disrupted scapulothoracic rhythm (scapular winging, elevation, tipping) is the most common shoulder movement dysfunction in rheumatologic and degenerative shoulder conditions.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — shoulder girdle strengthening; glenohumeral and scapulothoracic rehabilitation.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Rheumatoid Arthritis / Shoulder involvement (A003) Shoulder joint strengthening; rotator cuff activation
Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis) — subacute phase Progressively restore shoulder strength as range improves
Post-surgery shoulder rehabilitation Gradual functional strengthening
Cervical Spondylosis (A004 related) Shoulder girdle stabilisation reduces compensatory cervical strain

6. Contraindications

  • Acute shoulder joint inflammation: reduce to gentle range
  • Acute rotator cuff tear: avoid resistance exercises until healing is established
  • Post-surgery: follow specific protocol guidelines

7. Series Context

Sixth in the Sakti Vikasaka sequence. Preceded by Kaphoni (P0141); followed by Griva (P0143).
