Wrist Up and Down

Wrist Up and Down (Manibandha Spandana)

A focused variation of wrist movement emphasising isolated wrist extension (dorsiflexion) with the arm in different positions. Specifically addresses the dorsal wrist compartment and the extensor tendons — the structures most affected in repetitive strain and computer use injury.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Seated. Arms extended forward or bent at elbows.

Practice — Form 1 (arms extended forward):

  1. Extend both arms forward, palms facing down.
  2. Drop both wrists — palmarflexion. Then raise both wrists upward — dorsiflexion.
  3. Perform 10 rounds, emphasising full upward (dorsiflexion) range.

Practice — Form 2 (arms by sides):

  1. Let both arms hang loosely by the sides.
  2. Flex wrists forward (palmarflexion), then extend wrists backward (dorsiflexion).
  3. The hanging position allows greater passive elongation of the forearm muscles.

Breathing: Exhale on dorsiflexion (upward); inhale on palmarflexion (downward).

Duration: 10 rounds.

2. Anatomical Activation

Same as P0016 but with specific emphasis on:

  • Extensor carpi radialis longus and brevis: the primary wrist extensors; targeted in the emphasis on full dorsiflexion
  • Extensor digitorum communis: participates in wrist extension when fingers are extended simultaneously
  • Forearm extensor muscles are particularly worked in this variation

3. Physiological Effects

Extensor compartment focus

The extensor tendons of the wrist pass through the extensor retinaculum in six compartments along the dorsum of the wrist. These tendons are commonly affected in repetitive strain injuries (especially in keyboard users, musicians, and manual workers) due to friction within their synovial sheaths from repetitive small-range movements. The full-range wrist extension in this exercise moves the tendons through their maximum gliding range — stimulating synovial sheath lubrication and preventing intra-sheath adhesions.

Comparison to P0016

P0016 trains both palmarflexion and dorsiflexion equally. P0017 specifically emphasises dorsiflexion — making it the more appropriate exercise when extensor tendon pathology (dorsal wrist tenosynovitis, extensor tendinopathy) is the primary concern. In standard IAYT protocols, P0017 may replace or complement P0016.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — extensor tendon sheath conditioning; wrist dorsiflexion range maintenance.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Dorsal Wrist Tenosynovitis Extensor tendon gliding through full range
Repetitive Strain (keyboard use) Dorsal extensor compartment rehabilitation
Arthritis — Wrist (A003) Wrist ROM maintenance
All IAYT protocols Wrist preparation

6. Contraindications

Acute wrist injury — as per P0016.

7. Variations & Progressions

Combined with finger extension: Simultaneously extend both wrists and spread fingers widely — elongates the entire dorsal forearm-wrist-finger extensor chain.

Sequencing context: Companion to P0016. Together these two wrist exercises cover all forearm wrist muscle groups before progressing to P0018 (Elbow Bending).
