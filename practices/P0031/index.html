Hip Rotation

Hip Rotation (Kati Chakrasana — Shroni)

Pelvic circumduction in standing — rotating the pelvis in large circles around the fixed axis of the lumbar spine and hip joints. Mobilises the hip joints, iliopsoas, piriformis, and lumbosacral articulation through the full available range of pelvic motion.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana with feet hip-width apart.

Practice:

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart. Place hands on the hips (iliac crests).
  2. Begin rotating the pelvis in a large clockwise circle — lead with the pelvis: forward (anterior tilt), right, backward (posterior tilt), left, and back to forward. The upper body remains relatively still; the movement originates from the waist and hips.
  3. Make the circle as large and smooth as possible.
  4. Perform 10 rounds clockwise.
  5. Reverse: 10 rounds anti-clockwise.
  6. Relax in Sithila Tadasana.

Breathing: Normal throughout; the movement is continuous.

Duration: 10 rounds each direction.

2. Anatomical Activation

Muscles cyclically engaged during pelvic circumduction

  • Anterior tilt arc: iliopsoas (hip flexion pulling lumbar spine into lordosis); erector spinae (lumbar extension)
  • Right lateral shift arc: right quadratus lumborum; right hip abductors
  • Posterior tilt arc: gluteus maximus and hamstrings (hip extension and posterior pelvic tilt); rectus abdominis (anterior pelvic tilt resistance)
  • Left lateral shift arc: left quadratus lumborum; left hip abductors
  • Deep hip rotators (piriformis, obturator internus/externus, gemelli): continuously active throughout the circumduction maintaining femoral head centration

3. Physiological Effects

Hip joint mobilisation — weight-bearing circumduction

Pelvic circumduction in standing provides weight-bearing hip joint mobilisation — the femoral head articulates against the acetabulum throughout the arc. This weight-bearing synovial fluid distribution is complementary to the non-weight-bearing hip mobilisation in Pavanamuktasana (P0090). The compression-decompression cycling during the circle improves cartilage nutrition at the hip in the functional weight-bearing context.

Iliopsoas and piriformis conditioning

The iliopsoas (hip flexor) and piriformis (primary hip external rotator) are the two muscles most involved in hip function and most commonly shortened in sedentary individuals. Pelvic circumduction cyclically activates and lengthens both muscles — the iliopsoas is maximally elongated during the posterior pelvic tilt arc, and the piriformis is elongated during the internal rotation component of each circle.

Lumbosacral junction mobilisation

The lumbosacral joint (L5–S1 articulation) is the most loaded and most commonly arthritic segment of the lumbar spine. Pelvic circumduction distributes synovial fluid to the L5–S1 facet joints through the combined flexion-extension-rotation-lateral flexion pattern of the full circle.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — hip joint mobilisation; iliopsoas and piriformis conditioning; lumbosacral joint mobilisation; gluteal and hip abductor activation.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the pelvic region houses the muladhara and svadhisthana chakras; pelvic movement is understood to activate the pranic energy of these lower energy centres.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Hip (A003) Weight-bearing hip circumduction; synovial fluid distribution in functional position
Back Pain — Lumbosacral (A004) Lumbosacral joint mobilisation; iliopsoas lengthening
Diabetes (A001) Pelvis-spine stimulation of abdominal and pelvic organs
Constipation / GID Pelvic movement improves descending colon and sigmoid colon motility
All IAYT protocols (preparatory) Hip and lumbosacral preparation for all postures

6. Contraindications

  • Acute hip arthritis — weight-bearing circumduction may aggravate; rest until subacute
  • Acute lumbar disc herniation — combined rotation and anterior tilt loads the posterior disc; avoid in acute phase
  • Severe hip osteoarthritis — reduce range; perform within pain-free range only

7. Variations & Progressions

Standard (hands on hips, full circle): As described.

Hands extended (variation): Arms in horizontal plane — increases the leverage and rotational demand.

Sequencing context: Follows P0030 (Waist Rotation). Trunk group continues with P0032–P0035.
