Half Butterfly

Half Butterfly (Ardha Titali Asana)

Unilateral hip external rotation mobilisation in seated position — one knee bent with the foot on the opposite thigh, the knee bouncing gently toward the floor. The first hip opening exercise in the supine/seated hip mobilisation group.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Dandasana (seated with legs extended).

Practice:

  1. Sit in Dandasana. Bend the right knee, placing the right foot on the left thigh (near the groin) — the right knee points to the right.
  2. Hold the right knee with the right hand. Place the left hand on the left knee.
  3. Gently bounce the right knee toward the floor — small, rhythmic up-down movements. Do not force. Let the hip joint relax progressively.
  4. Perform 10–20 gentle bounces. Then hold the knee down as far as comfortable for 5 breaths.
  5. Repeat on the left side.

Breathing: Normal throughout; exhale on each gentle downward press.

Duration: 10–20 bounces + 5-breath hold per side.

2. Anatomical Activation

Primary structures

  • Hip external rotators (piriformis, obturator internus/externus, gemelli, quadratus femoris): lengthened by the hip external rotation position — gently stretched by the downward knee press
  • Adductor muscles (adductor longus, magnus, gracilis): stretched in the externally rotated, abducted position
  • Hip joint capsule (inferior-posterior): elongated in external rotation

3. Physiological Effects

Hip external rotator elongation

The hip external rotators are the deep muscles most commonly shortened in sedentary individuals (sitting in internal rotation postures) and in those with piriformis syndrome. Half Butterfly provides a gentle, progressive elongation of these structures through the combined hip external rotation and abduction position.

Adductor mobilisation — inner thigh

The adductors are stretched when the knee is pushed toward the floor — the thigh is in external rotation, abduction, and hip flexion, which maximally elongates the adductor group. This is the gentlest way to introduce adductor stretching, using only the weight of the knee and the practitioner's own hand as resistance.

Groin preparation for seated practices

Half Butterfly is specifically used in IAYT protocols as preparation for seated asanas (Padmasana, Baddha Konasana, Sukhasana) — reducing the groin and inner thigh resistance that otherwise makes these practices uncomfortable.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — hip external rotator elongation; adductor lengthening; hip joint capsule mobilisation in external rotation.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the opening of the inner thigh and groin is associated with opening the svadhisthana and muladhara chakra regions in yogic anatomy.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Hip (A003) Hip external rotation ROM maintenance; adductor mobility
Back Pain (A004) Hip external rotator mobility; piriformis lengthening
Piriformis Syndrome Direct piriformis elongation through external rotation
Preparation for Seated Practices Groin and inner thigh opening before Padmasana, Siddhasana, etc.
All IAYT protocols Standard hip opening — preparatory before supine practices

6. Contraindications

  • Acute hip pathology — avoid forced external rotation
  • Knee ligamentous injury — the crossed-leg position puts torsional stress on the medial structures of the crossed knee; be mindful of medial knee pain

7. Variations & Progressions

Stationary hold (less dynamic): Hold the external rotation position for 30–60 seconds rather than bouncing.

Full Butterfly (P0047): The bilateral version — both knees bent with feet together; greater groin opening.

Sequencing context: First hip opening exercise. Progresses to P0047 (Full Butterfly).
