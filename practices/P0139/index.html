Kara Prista Sakti Vikasaka (Back of Hand Strengthening)

Kara = hand, prista = back/posterior, sakti = strength, vikasaka = developer. The back-of-hand strengthening exercise of the Sakti Vikasaka series. Targets the dorsal aspect of the hand and the muscles responsible for finger extension and wrist extension — the group most commonly weakened in rheumatoid arthritis, where the typical deformity pattern involves finger flexor dominance and extensor weakness.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana or seated in Dandasana.

Practice:

  1. Extend both arms forward at shoulder level, palms facing downward (dorsal surface of the hands facing up).
  2. Make a loose fist — the knuckles (metacarpophalangeal joints) should be prominent on the back of the hand.
  3. With the back of the hands facing upward, perform strong isometric resistance exercises:
    • Push the back of the hands upward forcefully against imaginary resistance
    • Alternately press the dorsal surface of the hands together in opposition (back of right hand pressing against back of left hand and vice versa)
  4. Maintain maximum tension for 10–15 seconds.
  5. Relax and repeat 3–5 rounds.

Alternative technique (wrist extension with pronated arms):

  1. With arms extended forward, palms now facing downward.
  2. Extend the wrists forcefully (bend the hand upward, toward the ceiling, back of the hand leading).
  3. Hold 5–10 seconds and release.
  4. Repeat 10 times.

Notes:

  • The emphasis is specifically on the dorsal aspect of the hand — the extensor mechanisms.
  • Normal breathing throughout.

2. Anatomical Activation

Primary muscles

  • Extensor digitorum: the primary finger extensor; strengthened by the dorsal-hand resistance exercises
  • Extensor indicis and extensor digiti minimi: strengthen individual finger extension
  • Extensor carpi radialis and ulnaris: wrist extensors activated in the wrist-extension component
  • Lumbricants and dorsal interossei: the dorsal intrinsic muscles activated in the extended-finger position

The "dorsal" emphasis

Rheumatoid arthritis characteristically produces:

  1. Ulnar deviation of the fingers (lateral drift of the metacarpophalangeal joints)
  2. Boutonnière or swan-neck deformity (imbalance between flexor and extensor tendons)
  3. Extensor tendon weakness relative to the flexors

Kara Prista Sakti Vikasaka directly addresses the extensor weakness by specifically loading the dorsal hand musculature, countering the flexor-dominant imbalance.

3. Physiological Effects

Extensor-flexor balance restoration

By specifically strengthening the dorsal extensor group, this exercise progressively corrects the flexor dominance pattern that perpetuates ulnar deviation and joint deformity in RA.

Dorsal venous and lymphatic drainage

The isometric contraction of the dorsal hand muscles pumps the dorsal venous arcade and subcutaneous lymphatics of the hand — reducing the dorsal hand oedema that is characteristic of acute RA exacerbations.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — dorsal hand and finger extensor strengthening; extensor-flexor balance.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Rheumatoid Arthritis (A003) Primary — extensor strength to counter flexor-dominant deformity
Osteoarthritis of the hands General hand extensor maintenance
Post-stroke hand rehabilitation Extensor re-strengthening (extensors typically weaker than flexors post-stroke)

6. Contraindications

  • Acute arthritis flare: reduce intensity
  • Active tendon rupture (extensor digitorum): rest until healed

7. Series Context

Third in the Sakti Vikasaka sequence. Preceded by Kara Tala (P0138); followed by Mani Bandha (P0140).
