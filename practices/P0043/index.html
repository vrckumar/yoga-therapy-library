Alternate Straight Leg Raising

Alternate Straight Leg Raising (Vikalpa Uttanpadasana)

Alternating single-leg raises — right leg up while left is down, then left up while right is down. The standard bilateral form of P0042. Adds a coordination and stabilisation challenge as the spine must adjust to the alternating hip flexor pull from each side.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Supine Posture.

Practice:

  1. Lie flat. While inhaling, raise the right leg to 90°.
  2. While exhaling, lower the right leg.
  3. While inhaling, raise the left leg to 90°.
  4. While exhaling, lower the left leg.
  5. This is one round. Perform 10 rounds.

Breathing: Inhale on raising; exhale on lowering.

Duration: 10 rounds (alternating right-left).

2. Anatomical Activation

Same as P0042 but alternating:

  • Asymmetric hip flexor activation creates an alternating lateral spinal loading that requires the obliques and quadratus lumborum to provide counter-stabilisation
  • The contralateral leg on the floor provides a grounding effect and prevents pelvic rotation

3. Physiological Effects

Alternating hip flexor activation — bilateral balance assessment

The alternating form reveals any side-to-side difference in hip flexor strength, hip joint mobility, or neural tension — one side may raise more easily than the other. This asymmetry is clinically significant in back pain (often associated with the symptomatic side having reduced strength or greater neural tension) and in functional rehabilitation.

Oblique stabilisation

The alternating hip flexor pull requires the obliques to activate alternately — the left obliques stabilise when the right leg raises, and vice versa. This alternating stabilisation pattern is the core activation mechanism most relevant to gait (walking alternates limb loading on each step).

See P0042 for all other physiological mechanisms.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — bilateral hip flexor strengthening with asymmetric core stabilisation; functional walking-pattern simulation.

5. Indicated For

Same as P0042. The alternating form is the standard version and is appropriate for most patients.

6. Contraindications

Same as P0042.

7. Variations & Progressions

Speed variation: Can be performed slowly (breath-synchronised) or more rapidly for cardiovascular effect.

Progression: After mastering alternating, progress to P0044 (both legs simultaneously) — for non-back-pain patients.

Sequencing context: Standard form of straight leg raising. Follows P0042; progresses to P0044.
