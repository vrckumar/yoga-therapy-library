Wrist Movement

Wrist Movement (Manibandha Chakra)

Full wrist flexion and extension — the sagittal-plane counterpart to wrist circumduction (P0015). Isolates the palmarflexion-dorsiflexion arc to maximise range and forearm muscle activation in the primary wrist movement plane.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Seated. Arms extended forward.

Practice:

  1. Extend both arms forward at shoulder height, palms facing down.
  2. Flex both wrists downward (palmarflexion) as far as possible — fingers point toward the floor.
  3. Extend both wrists upward (dorsiflexion) as far as possible — fingers point toward the ceiling.
  4. This is one round. Perform 10 rounds.

Variation: Perform with palms facing up for a slightly different muscle emphasis.

Breathing: Exhale on palmarflexion; inhale on dorsiflexion.

Duration: 10 rounds.

2. Anatomical Activation

Palmarflexion (wrist flexion)

  • Flexor carpi radialis: primary wrist flexor; also radially deviates
  • Flexor carpi ulnaris: primary wrist flexor; also ulnarly deviates
  • Palmaris longus: wrist flexion and palmar aponeurosis tension

Dorsiflexion (wrist extension)

  • Extensor carpi radialis longus and brevis: primary wrist extensors; also radially deviate
  • Extensor carpi ulnaris: wrist extension with ulnar deviation

3. Physiological Effects

Radiocarpal joint sagittal mobilisation

The sagittal (palmarflexion-dorsiflexion) plane is the primary wrist movement plane and the one most affected by stiffness. Full palmarflexion (approximately 80°) stretches the dorsal radiocarpal ligaments and extensor tendons; full dorsiflexion (approximately 70°) stretches the volar radiocarpal ligaments and flexor tendons. The repeated cycling through both extremes distributes synovial fluid across the entire radiocarpal articular surface and conditions the limiting ligaments.

Forearm flexor-extensor balance

Wrist overuse conditions (tennis elbow, golfer's elbow, repetitive strain) frequently involve imbalance between the forearm flexors (stronger, more active in daily use) and extensors (weaker, undertrained). The wrist movement exercise trains both groups in equal measure — each movement activating one group concentrically and the other eccentrically — restoring the forearm muscle balance.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — radiocarpal sagittal mobilisation; forearm flexor-extensor conditioning; tendon sheath gliding.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Wrist (A003) Primary wrist plane mobilisation
Lateral Epicondylitis (Tennis Elbow) Wrist extensor conditioning; reduces tendon overload
Medial Epicondylitis (Golfer's Elbow) Wrist flexor conditioning
Carpal Tunnel Syndrome Flexor tendon gliding through full range
All IAYT protocols (preparatory) Wrist preparation before weight-bearing practices

6. Contraindications

  • Acute wrist sprain — avoid extremes of range
  • Acute carpal tunnel — moderate range, avoid extremes of palmarflexion

7. Variations & Progressions

With fists (variation): Performing the movement with closed fists increases finger flexor and extensor participation.

Sequencing context: Follows P0015 (circumduction); together these two constitute the complete wrist series. Progresses to P0017 (Wrist Up and Down — variations) then P0018 (Elbow Bending).
