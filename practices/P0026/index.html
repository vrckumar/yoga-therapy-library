Neck Movement

Neck Movement (Greeva Sanchalana — Sampoorna)

Combined cervical movements — a comprehensive sequence that progresses through flexion, extension, bilateral rotation, and bilateral lateral flexion in sequence. Provides the full cervical mobilisation in a single organised practice when the complete individual exercises (P0023–P0025) are not practised separately.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana or seated. Spine erect, shoulders relaxed.

Practice:

  1. Forward and backward: 5 rounds (chin to chest / backward tilt) — as per P0023.
  2. Right and left rotation: 5 rounds alternating — as per P0024.
  3. Right and left lateral bending: 5 rounds alternating — as per P0025.
  4. Finally, perform 3–5 slow, full cervical circumduction circles — head leading in a smooth circle through all the above planes (forward, right side, backward, left side, and back to forward).
  5. Reverse circumduction: 3–5 circles.
  6. Relax.

Breathing: Exhale on each movement direction; inhale returning to neutral. During circumduction: breathe normally.

Duration: Approximately 3–4 minutes for the complete sequence.

2. Anatomical Activation

Combines all structures from P0023, P0024, and P0025:

  • All cervical muscles cyclically activated in all planes
  • All cervical facet joints mobilised (anterior, posterior, lateral)
  • All cervical intervertebral discs cycled through combined loading patterns

3. Physiological Effects

Comprehensive cervical mobilisation

The combined sequence provides 3D cervical joint mobilisation — sagittal, transverse, and frontal planes. The final circumduction circles combine all three planes simultaneously, achieving the most complete cervical joint surface coverage possible with a single movement.

Cervical proprioceptive retraining

The cervical spine contains a high density of proprioceptors (muscle spindles and joint mechanoreceptors) that provide the central nervous system with precise head position information — critical for gaze stabilisation, vestibular function, and postural control. Moving the cervical spine through all planes in a coordinated sequence stimulates these proprioceptors, maintaining their sensitivity and the cervicogenic contribution to balance and gaze control.

Clinical note on circumduction: Full cervical circumduction should be performed slowly and smoothly. Any dizziness during backward or lateral extension arcs suggests vertebral artery sensitivity — discontinue circumduction and use individual directional movements only.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — complete 3D cervical mobilisation; proprioceptive retraining; all cervical muscle groups conditioned.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Cervical Spondylosis / Arthritis (A003) Comprehensive cervical joint mobilisation when individual exercises are impractical
Cervicogenic Headache Complete cervical muscle elongation
All IAYT protocols Standard combined cervical exercise for general protocols

6. Contraindications

  • All contraindications from P0023–P0025 apply
  • Circumduction (circular): Avoid if vertebral artery symptoms occur; use individual directional movements instead
  • Cervical myelopathy or instability: Avoid circumduction; use restricted range individual movements only

7. Variations & Progressions

Without circumduction (modified): For elderly or spondylotic patients — perform only the three linear movements, omitting the circular component.

Sequencing context: Used as the standard combined cervical exercise in general IAYT protocols. Progresses to P0027 (Neck Movement and Rotation — further combined forms).
