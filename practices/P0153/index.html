Reiki Full Body Relaxation (Energy Healing Relaxation Session)

Rei (Japanese) = universal spirit / higher intelligence, ki = life-force energy (equivalent to prana in Sanskrit). A Reiki session is a form of energy-based complementary therapy in which a trained practitioner channels ki (universal life-force energy) through their hands — placing them lightly on or just above specific regions of the receiver's body to promote energetic balancing, release energetic blockages, and support the body's natural healing processes. In the SVYASA IAYT context, Reiki full-body relaxation sessions are used as an adjunct modality for deep Anandamaya Kosha (bliss body) relaxation and are included in select protocol modules where deep energetic healing is indicated.

External modality note: Reiki is not a yoga practice in the traditional sense; it originates from Japan (developed by Mikao Usui in the early 20th century). It is included in some IAYT protocols as a complementary modality aligned with the holistic, multi-kosha model of health. A trained Reiki practitioner is required to deliver this session.

1. Session Description

Format

A full-body Reiki relaxation session typically lasts 45–60 minutes. The receiver lies in Savasana (corpse pose) on a comfortable flat surface, fully clothed. Eyes are closed, breathing is natural, and the mind is invited to rest.

The practitioner's approach

The Reiki practitioner:

  1. Centres themselves with a brief meditation or prayer before beginning.
  2. Places their hands gently on or slightly above (hovering) a series of predetermined body positions — scanning from the head, through the torso, to the feet.
  3. At each position, the hands are maintained for 3–5 minutes, transmitting the Reiki energy with intention.
  4. The session concludes with the practitioner sealing the energy field and grounding the receiver.

Hand positions (standard full-body sequence)

  • Head: crown of the head, forehead, eyes, temples, back of the head
  • Throat and neck: front of the throat, back of the neck
  • Chest: upper chest (Anahata region), mid-chest
  • Abdomen: solar plexus (Manipura), lower abdomen (Svadhisthana)
  • Back: upper back, mid-back, lower back (sacrum)
  • Legs: thighs, knees, lower legs, feet

2. The Multi-Kosha Framework

In the IAYT five-kosha model, Reiki is specifically associated with the Anandamaya Kosha (bliss body) and Vijnanamaya Kosha (wisdom/intuitive body):

Kosha Reiki Effect
Annamaya (physical) Deep physical relaxation; muscle tension release via prolonged stillness
Pranamaya (vital energy) Direct pranic channel rebalancing — Reiki's stated mechanism of action
Manomaya (mental-emotional) Reduction in mental chatter; emotional clearing
Vijnanamaya (wisdom) Access to intuitive stillness; clarity
Anandamaya (bliss) The primary target — access to the deep restful bliss state beneath ordinary awareness

3. Physiological Correlates

The physiological effects of Reiki sessions as reported in the research literature (primarily from nursing and integrative oncology contexts):

  • Significant reduction in pain scores in post-operative and cancer patients
  • Heart rate and blood pressure reduction during and after sessions
  • Reduction in anxiety (trait and state) after repeated sessions
  • Improved sleep quality in patients with insomnia
  • Reduced fatigue in patients undergoing chemotherapy

The proposed mechanisms include:

  • The biofield theory (the practitioner's hands emit low-level electromagnetic fields that interact with the receiver's biofield)
  • Relaxation response (similar to DRT/Yoga Nidra) — the session format itself may produce a deep relaxation response regardless of the specific energy modality
  • Therapeutic presence and touch — the experience of being cared for by an attentive practitioner has measurable physiological benefits

4. Role in IAYT Protocols

Reiki full-body relaxation is used in IAYT when:

  1. The patient requires a deep Anandamaya Kosha intervention — typically in cases where conventional yoga relaxation techniques have not produced sufficient depth of rest
  2. An energetic rebalancing is indicated alongside the physical-pranic yoga practices
  3. The patient is open to and requests energy healing as part of their IAYT programme

It is typically offered as a one-off or periodic (once per week) session rather than a daily practice.

5. Kosha Level

Primary: Anandamaya Kosha — deep bliss-body activation through energy channel rebalancing.

Secondary: Vijnanamaya Kosha — the stillness of the session supports access to the intuitive witnessing awareness layer.

7. Contraindications

  • Pacemaker: electromagnetic field considerations; discuss with cardiologist
  • Active psychosis: energy-based work may be over-stimulating in acute psychotic states
  • Patient refusal or discomfort: Reiki should only be offered with informed consent and patient acceptance; never imposed

8. Practitioner Requirement

Reiki sessions must be delivered by a practitioner trained to at least Reiki Level II (Okuden) — the level at which distance and systematic full-body treatment protocols are taught. In the SVYASA IAYT context, Reiki is administered by a trained therapist, not self-administered by the patient.
