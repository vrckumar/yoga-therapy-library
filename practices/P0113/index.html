Loosen Yourself Sequence (Complete Sithilikarana Vyayama Session)

A meta-practice page describing the full Loosen Yourself (Sithilikarana Vyayama) session performed as a single continuous sequence. Individual component exercises are documented under P0001–P0056 (the Sithilikarana Vyayama series). This entry describes the purpose, structure, and clinical application of the complete loosening session as a therapeutic unit, along with the extended IAYT special techniques prescription that includes the loosening exercises as part of a broader vigorous movement protocol.

1. What This Sequence Is

The Sithilikarana Vyayama ("sithila" = loose/relaxed, "karana" = making, "vyayama" = exercise) are the complete set of joint-by-joint loosening, mobilisation, and warming exercises that begin every IAYT yoga therapy session. They progress systematically through:

  • Individual joint movements (toes, ankles, knees, hips, wrists, elbows, shoulders, neck)
  • Spinal movements (forward-backward bending, side bending, twisting)
  • Dynamic whole-body exercises (jumping, swimming movements, rocking and rolling)

Performed as a complete session, the sequence constitutes a thorough warm-up that:

  1. Mobilises every major joint in a safe, progressive sequence
  2. Generates sufficient body heat and circulation to prepare for the asana practice
  3. Begins the process of proprioceptive re-education in patients with reduced joint awareness
  4. Establishes the breath-body coordination rhythm for the session

2. Full Sequence Structure

Standing phase (Tadasana sthiti)

  1. Jogging (forward, backward, sideward) with Mukha Dhauti
  2. Forward-Backward Bending
  3. Side Bending
  4. Twisting (legs apart)
  5. Toe touching
  6. Heel touching
  7. Swimming movement
  8. Hip Rotation
  9. Knee Rotation
  10. Back Swing

Kneeling/floor transition

  1. Surya Namaskar (preparation)
  2. Pavanamuktasana Kriya

Supine phase (Savasana sthiti)

  1. Rocking and Rolling
  2. Alternate Knee Touching
  3. Lumbar Stretch (folded legs / crossed legs)
  4. QRT (2 minutes)

Seated phase (Dandasana sthiti)

  1. Butterfly (Half and Full — P0151)
  2. Ardha Padmasana Swing
  3. Paschimottanasana Stretch
  4. Halasana-Paschimottanasana Swing (P0150)
  5. Cycling (15 rounds)

Prone phase (Makarasana sthiti)

  1. Bhujangasana Breathing (P0066)
  2. Salabhasana (Boating — Bhujangasana, Salabhasana)
  3. Dhanurasana Swing (P0085)
  4. Straight Leg Rise (P0161)
  5. Side Leg Rising

Dynamic standing phase

  1. Naukasana / Navasana
  2. Tiger Stretch
  3. Back Stretch
  4. Side Stretch
  5. Free Walk, Baby Walk, Camel Walk, Crow Walk
  6. Frog Jump
  7. Step climbing

3. Sthiti (Starting and Relaxation Positions)

The Sithilikarana Vyayama system specifies precise starting and relaxation postures for each phase:

Phase Starting (Sthiti) Relaxation (Sithila)
Standing Tadasana (feet together, erect) Sithila Tadasana (feet apart, shoulders collapsed)
Seated Dandasana (legs extended, erect) Sithila Dandasana (legs apart, trunk slightly back)
Prone Prone Sthiti (legs together, arms overhead) Makarasana (feet apart, arms crossed under chin)
Supine Supine Sthiti (legs together, arms overhead) Savasana (legs and arms apart, collapsed)

Transitions between postures are always through these defined positions — this ensures the patient neither collapses nor over-extends between exercises.

4. Physiological Purpose

Joint-by-joint mobilisation

The progression from the smallest distal joints (toes) toward the large proximal joints (hips, spine) follows the therapeutic principle of warming the periphery before loading the centre. This reduces injury risk and ensures synovial fluid distribution throughout each joint before range-of-motion demands are placed on it.

Visceral warming

The dynamic components (jogging, jumping, vigorous swings) generate metabolic heat that:

  • Increases the temperature of abdominal viscera by 1–2°C
  • Accelerates enzymatic digestive activity
  • Prepares the organs for the direct stimulation in subsequent asanas

Psycho-physical readiness

The standardised sequence of the full loosening session serves as a reliable transitional ritual from the patient's daily mental activity into the focused, internally attentive state required for IAYT. The predictability of the sequence reduces cognitive load and allows the attention to settle naturally.

5. Duration and Clinical Use

Full session: 20–30 minutes when all components are performed.

Abbreviated session: 10–15 minutes for outpatient settings — typically the joint mobilisation phases without the vigorous dynamic phase.

Session opener: In all eight IAYT protocols (Diabetes, Obesity, Arthritis, Back Pain, Hypertension, Anxiety/Depression, Asthma, GID), the loosening exercises are prescribed as the first module of the session — regardless of the specific therapeutic focus.
