Kara Tala Sakti Vikasaka (Palm Strengthening)

Kara = hand, tala = palm, sakti = strength, vikasaka = developer. The palm-strengthening exercise of the Sakti Vikasaka series. The arms are stretched sideways at shoulder level, fingers spread and stretched maximally, and the palms are moved vigorously up and down from the wrist. This exercise specifically strengthens the wrist extensors and the intrinsic muscles of the palm, while stretching the palmar fascia and improving wrist mobility.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Tadasana.

Practice:

  1. Stretch both arms sideways at shoulder level, palms facing downward.
  2. Bend the elbows and bring the hands toward the chest.
  3. Spread out and stretch all the fingers as wide as possible.
  4. Bring the palms near the chest with fingers spread.
  5. Now move both palms vigorously upward and downward from the wrist — the wrist flexion-extension range is used rapidly and forcefully.
  6. Feel the strong tightness and stretch in the fingers and palms.
  7. Normal breathing throughout.
  8. Repeat 10–20 times.

2. Anatomical Activation

Primary muscles

  • Wrist extensors (extensor carpi radialis longus/brevis, extensor carpi ulnaris): concentrically active in the up-stroke (wrist extension)
  • Wrist flexors (flexor carpi radialis, flexor carpi ulnaris): concentrically active in the down-stroke (wrist flexion)
  • Intrinsic palmar muscles (lumbricants, interossei, thenar and hypothenar groups): activated by the finger-spread position
  • Palmar fascia: stretched by the full finger extension in the spread position

The palm emphasis

The combination of fingers-spread (maximum digital abduction) with vigorous wrist movement provides a more comprehensive exercise for the entire palmar complex than pure finger exercises alone. The thenar eminence (thumb base muscles: abductor pollicis brevis, flexor pollicis brevis, opponens pollicis) and the hypothenar group are specifically activated by the forced digit-spreading.

3. Physiological Effects

Intrinsic hand muscle rehabilitation

The thenar and hypothenar muscles are among the most commonly weakened in rheumatoid arthritis, peripheral neuropathy (e.g., carpal tunnel syndrome), and stroke. This exercise:

  • Strengthens these muscles through the finger-spread and wrist-movement cycle
  • Improves the proprioceptive input from the palm mechanoreceptors (Meissner's corpuscles, Ruffini endings) through the vigorous movement

Wrist joint lubrication

The vigorous wrist flexion-extension cycle distributes synovial fluid through the radiocarpal and midcarpal joints — addressing the morning wrist stiffness common in RA.

Palmar fascia stretching

The finger-spread position places the palmar fascia under stretch — relevant for Dupuytren's contracture and the fascia tightening associated with chronic RA.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — intrinsic hand muscle strengthening; wrist mobilisation; palmar fascia stretching.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Rheumatoid Arthritis / Hand OA Intrinsic hand muscle strengthening; wrist joint mobilisation
Carpal tunnel syndrome (subacute) Wrist mobility and tendon gliding
Post-stroke hand rehabilitation Thenar and intrinsic muscle reactivation

6. Contraindications

  • Acute RA flare in the hands: reduce intensity; gentle range only
  • Active tenosynovitis: avoid forceful wrist movement until inflammation subsides

7. Series Context

Second in the Sakti Vikasaka sequence. Preceded by Anguli Sakti Vikasaka (P0137); followed by Kara Prista Sakti Vikasaka (P0139).
