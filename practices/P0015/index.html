Loosening of Wrists

Loosening of Wrists (Manibandha Naman)

Full wrist circumduction — circular rotation of the wrist joint that takes the radiocarpal and midcarpal joints through their combined range of motion in all planes simultaneously. The wrist equivalent of ankle rotation (P0004).

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Seated. Arms extended forward, parallel to the floor, elbows straight. Make loose fists or keep hands open.

Practice:

  1. Extend both arms forward at shoulder height.
  2. Rotate both wrists simultaneously in large, smooth clockwise circles — the maximum circle the wrist can make. Perform 10 rounds clockwise.
  3. Reverse: 10 rounds anti-clockwise.
  4. Release and shake the hands gently.

Breathing: Normal throughout; the circular motion is continuous.

Duration: 10 rounds each direction.

2. Anatomical Activation

Muscles cyclically activated during circumduction

  • Dorsiflexion arc: extensor carpi radialis longus and brevis; extensor carpi ulnaris
  • Ulnar deviation arc: extensor carpi ulnaris; flexor carpi ulnaris
  • Palmarflexion arc: flexor carpi radialis; flexor carpi ulnaris; palmaris longus
  • Radial deviation arc: extensor carpi radialis longus; flexor carpi radialis

Joints mobilised

  • Radiocarpal joint: primary wrist joint between the radius and the proximal carpal row (scaphoid, lunate, triquetrum)
  • Midcarpal joint: between proximal and distal carpal rows — contributes to wrist ROM
  • Distal radioulnar joint: mild participation during forearm pronation-supination component of circumduction

3. Physiological Effects

Carpal joint mobilisation

The wrist is a complex of eight carpal bones arranged in two rows, with intricate inter-carpal ligaments and joint capsules. Restricted wrist mobility often results from tightening of these inter-carpal ligaments — particularly the dorsal radiocarpal ligament and the radioscapholunate ligament. Wrist circumduction provides the most comprehensive mobilisation of these ligaments by cycling through all planes.

Tendon sheath mobilisation — six tendon compartments

The dorsum of the wrist contains six extensor tendon compartments enclosed in synovial sheaths. The flexor tendons pass through the carpal tunnel beneath the flexor retinaculum. All of these tendons must glide freely within their sheaths for normal wrist and finger function. Wrist circumduction moves all tendons through their full gliding range, preventing intra-sheath adhesions — the mechanism of carpal tunnel syndrome prevention.

De Quervain's tenosynovitis prevention

De Quervain's tenosynovitis affects the first extensor compartment (abductor pollicis longus and extensor pollicis brevis). Wrist circumduction includes ulnar deviation — a movement that specifically loads and then releases these tendons through their sheath, maintaining gliding mobility.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — radiocarpal and midcarpal mobilisation; all six dorsal extensor compartment and carpal tunnel tendon gliding; inter-carpal ligament conditioning.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the wrists are the secondary prana transmission points of the upper limb (after the hands); loosening the wrist joints removes blocks to prana flow toward the hands.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Wrist (A003) Comprehensive carpal joint mobilisation; synovial fluid distribution
Carpal Tunnel Syndrome Tendon gliding maintenance; reduction of tunnel pressure
Repetitive Strain / Occupational Wrist Overuse Inter-carpal ligament mobilisation; tendon sheath conditioning
De Quervain's Tenosynovitis (subacute) First extensor compartment tendon gliding; reduces sheath adhesion
All IAYT protocols (preparatory) Wrist preparation before weight-bearing on hands (plank, cat, etc.)

6. Contraindications

  • Acute wrist sprain (ligamentous) — avoid circumduction during acute healing; early protected ROM within pain-free range after assessment
  • Carpal tunnel syndrome (acute, severe) — extreme wrist positions (full flexion or extension) may worsen median nerve compression; moderate range only
  • Wrist fracture (acute) — follow surgical/cast protocol for ROM timing

7. Variations & Progressions

Bilateral simultaneous (standard): As described.

Interlocked hands (variation): Interlock the fingers of both hands and rotate both wrists together — adds inter-carpal mobilisation through the interlocked position.

Sequencing context: Follows P0014 (Loosening of Fingers). Progresses to P0016 (Wrist Movement — linear). Together these constitute the complete wrist mobilisation sequence.
