Chair Relaxation (Seated Savasana / Chair QRT)

The chair adaptation of the full-body relaxation practices — Instant Relaxation Technique (IRT), Quick Relaxation Technique (QRT), and Deep Relaxation Technique (DRT) — performed while seated in a chair. Intended as the Savasana equivalent for patients who cannot lie on the floor. Used as the opening step (Step 1) of the Yoga-Chair-Breathing protocol, and as the closing relaxation for all chair yoga sessions. Provides complete physical and mental relaxation while in a supported seated position.

1. Technique

Chair IRT (Instant Relaxation Technique — seated)

Starting position: Sit on the floor with legs stretched under the chair. Pull the chair toward the chest and hold onto the chair seat with both arms. Alternatively, sit on the chair itself.

Practice:

  1. Start tightening from the toes — tense the toes, ankles, calves.
  2. Pull up the kneecaps and tighten the thigh muscles.
  3. Compress and squeeze the buttocks.
  4. Breathe out and suck the abdomen in.
  5. Form fists and tighten the arms.
  6. Inhale and expand the chest.
  7. Tighten the shoulders, neck muscles, and compress the face.
  8. Tighten the whole body from toes to head simultaneously — tighten, tighten, tighten.
  9. Release the whole body instantaneously — collapse completely against the chair. Let the arms fall, the head drop forward, the legs fall apart.
  10. Rest the head and arms on the chair seat (if sitting on the floor in front of the chair) or rest the arms on the armrests and let the trunk slump slightly.
  11. Enjoy the instant release of tension.

Duration: 30–60 seconds for the tightening-release cycle. Rest for 2–5 minutes afterward.

Chair QRT (Quick Relaxation Technique — seated)

Starting position: Sit in the chair with the trunk slightly forward, arms resting on the thighs or hanging loosely. Eyes closed.

Practice:

Phase I: Feel the abdominal movements. Observe the rise and fall of the abdomen with normal breathing for 5 cycles.

Phase II: Synchronise the abdominal movements with deep breathing — abdomen rises with inhalation, sinks with exhalation. Observe 5 deep, slow cycles.

Phase III: On each inhalation, feel the body energise. On each exhalation, collapse the muscles further and enjoy the relaxation. Optionally chant "AAA" softly on the exhalation, feeling the vibrations in the lower body. Observe 5 cycles.

Chair DRT (Deep Relaxation — extended version)

For longer sessions (10–20 minutes), a systematic body scan is performed while seated:

  1. Progressively bring awareness through the toes, feet, legs, hips, abdomen, chest, back, arms, shoulders, neck, face.
  2. At each region, consciously release any holding or tension.
  3. End with breath awareness for 3–5 minutes.

2. Anatomical Activation

IRT mechanism

The deliberate maximum tightening of all muscle groups simultaneously:

  • Creates maximum isometric contraction of the somatic musculature
  • Followed by the simultaneous release — the release is more complete because it follows maximum voluntary contraction (the post-contraction inhibition mechanism, also called reciprocal inhibition)
  • Equivalent to the PMR (Progressive Muscle Relaxation) technique of Jacobson

QRT mechanism

The synchronisation of abdominal movement with breath awareness:

  • Activates the respiratory-autonomic feedback loop
  • The deliberate slow breathing slows the heart rate through baroreceptor and vagal pathways
  • The body scan reduces sympathetic muscle tone systematically

3. Physiological Effects

Sympathetic nervous system downregulation

Both IRT and QRT produce measurable reductions in sympathetic activation markers:

  • Heart rate slows
  • Blood pressure drops
  • Peripheral vascular resistance decreases (vasodilation)
  • Electromyographic activity decreases in the skeletal muscles

Chair adaptation benefits

For patients who cannot lie supine (COPD, severe GERD, orthopnoea, post-surgical positions), the chair relaxation achieves equivalent physiological downregulation while respecting the postural restriction. In the Yoga-Chair-Breathing protocol, the chair IRT is specifically designed to initiate relaxation during an asthma attack — when lying down would worsen the breathlessness.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — somatic muscle relaxation; autonomic downregulation.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the QRT breath synchronisation normalises the pranic flow throughout the body.

Tertiary: Manomaya Kosha — the body scan and breath awareness techniques directly address the anxious mental activity that perpetuates physical tension.

6. Contraindications

  • No significant contraindications for the QRT
  • IRT (tightening phase): avoid in patients with very high blood pressure (>180/110), recent cardiac event, or severe musculoskeletal pain — use QRT or DRT instead
