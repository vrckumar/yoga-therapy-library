Chair Sasankasana Movement (Forward Bend in Chair)

The Sasankasana (child's pose / forward bend from Vajrasana) adapted for use with a chair. The patient sits in Vajrasana (or approximates it on the heels) and performs forward-backward bending from the waist using the chair as a guide and support — an equivalent of the floor-based Sasankasana Breathing (P0061), but with the chair providing a resting surface for the head and arms in the forward fold. Used as Step 4 of the Yoga-Chair-Breathing protocol for acute asthma management. Also used in general chair yoga sessions for lumbar decompression when floor-kneeling is unavailable.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Sit in Vajrasana (on heels) in front of the chair, or seated on the chair edge. The chair seat is in front or to the side.

Form A (Vajrasana in front of chair):

  1. Sit on the heels in Vajrasana, facing the chair.
  2. Hold the right wrist behind the back with the left hand (as in standard Sasankasana Breathing).
  3. Sasankasana movement only (5 times): bend forward and backward from the waist without breath synchronisation — simply moving the torso in both directions freely.
  4. Sasankasana movement with breathing (5 times): synchronise — inhale while leaning back, exhale while bending forward. In the forward fold, the forehead or forearms may rest on the chair seat.
  5. Sasankasana movement with M-kara (5 times): chant the "M" sound (MMM humming) in a low voice while bending forward. The vibration resonates in the chest.

Form B (seated on chair edge):

  1. Sit on the front edge of the chair, feet flat on the floor.
  2. Lean forward from the waist, letting the trunk fold over the thighs.
  3. Arms hang loosely beside the legs or rest on the thighs.
  4. The position approximates a seated Sasankasana forward fold.
  5. Synchronise with breathing — inhale as the trunk rises, exhale as it folds forward.

2. Anatomical Activation

Same as Sasankasana Breathing (P0061):

  • Lumbar erector spinae: eccentrically lengthened in the forward fold
  • Posterior hip muscles: stretched in the seated forward fold
  • Thoracolumbar fascia: stretched in the forward position
  • Abdominal organs: compressed between thighs and trunk in the forward fold

Chair-specific additions:

  • Head and arms supported: the chair seat bears the weight of the head and forearms in the deep forward fold — this reduces cervical and upper limb effort, allowing deeper relaxation into the fold
  • Psychological safety: the chair provides a physical boundary for the forward fold, allowing patients with anxiety about falling forward to relax into the position

3. Physiological Effects

Lumbar decompression (same as Sasankasana Breathing)

The forward fold from Vajrasana decompresses the lumbar spine and stretches the posterior chain — same mechanism as P0061. The chair adaptation makes this benefit available to patients who cannot comfortably kneel or sit cross-legged on the floor.

M-kara vibration therapy in asthma

The "M-kara" humming on exhalation during the forward fold:

  • Creates thoracic vibration that helps mobilise bronchial secretions
  • The extended humming exhalation slows the respiratory rate significantly
  • Stimulates the vagus nerve branches in the chest through mechanical vibration — the same mechanism as humming-based Bhramari Pranayama
  • In the acute asthma context (Yoga-Chair-Breathing Step 4), this is the critical step for reducing bronchospasm

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — lumbar decompression; posterior chain stretching.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the forward fold with exhalation deepens apana vayu; M-kara vibration stimulates the thoracic pranic field.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Asthma (A007) Primary — Step 4 of Yoga-Chair-Breathing; M-kara vibration reduces bronchospasm
Back Pain (A004) Lumbar decompression accessible in chair
Anxiety (A006) Forward fold parasympathetic activation
Elderly / mobility-restricted patients Chair adaptation for those who cannot kneel on the floor

6. Contraindications

  • Severe GERD / Hiatal hernia: the forward fold increases intra-abdominal pressure; may worsen reflux
  • Late pregnancy: trunk forward fold may be uncomfortable; avoid deep compression
  • Vertigo: the forward head position may aggravate; maintain a higher head position
