Knee Stretch

Knee Stretch (Janu Prishtha Naman)

Supine knee flexion and extension — a gentle, supported mobilisation of the knee joint from the supine position. Complements the standing knee exercises (P0009–P0011) with a gravity-neutral approach that reduces axial joint loading.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Supine Posture.

Practice:

  1. Lie flat. Support the right thigh with both hands (hold behind the thigh, just above the knee).
  2. With the thigh supported at approximately 90° hip flexion, slowly flex the right knee further — drawing the heel toward the buttock.
  3. Then gently extend the knee — leg hanging down from the supported thigh.
  4. Perform 10 flexion-extension cycles gently, with breath coordination.
  5. Repeat on the left.

Breathing: Exhale on flexion; inhale on extension.

Duration: 10 repetitions per leg.

2. Anatomical Activation

  • Hamstrings: concentrically active in knee flexion
  • Quadriceps: concentrically active in knee extension
  • Gastrocnemius: assists knee flexion
  • Popliteus: rotates the tibia at the start of flexion
  • Knee joint capsule: mobilised with reduced compression compared to standing

3. Physiological Effects

Low-load knee mobilisation

By supporting the thigh in a horizontal position, the knee joint is mobilised without the full axial compressive load of weight-bearing. The tibiofemoral joint is cycled through its range under partial gravity — the leg's weight below the knee provides gentle resistance without the full body weight. This makes knee stretch specifically valuable for patients with significant knee arthritis or post-surgical knees that cannot tolerate weight-bearing knee bending.

Hamstring facilitation in shortened position

With the hip flexed at 90° and the knee flexing, the hamstrings are contracting from a position where they are already partially shortened (hip flexion reduces their length). This mid-range hamstring activation — contracting from a shortened position — is a different stimulus from the elongated hamstring loading of standing knee flexion.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — low-load tibiofemoral joint mobilisation; hamstring activation in mid-range; synovial fluid distribution.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Knee (A003) Low-load knee ROM; safe for significantly arthritic knees
Post-knee surgery Gentle, supported ROM in early rehabilitation
Knee Stiffness without Weight-bearing Capacity Supine mobilisation when standing is too painful
Back Pain (A004) Gentle hamstring and knee mobilisation in supine

6. Contraindications

  • Acute hamstring strain — active contraction during acute phase
  • Acute knee effusion — reduce range

7. Variations & Progressions

Passive stretching (hold at end range): At maximum comfortable flexion, hold for 5 breaths — passive hamstring stretch.

Sequencing context: Part of the supine knee mobilisation subgroup. Progresses to P0051 (Lumbar Stretch — both knees to chest).
