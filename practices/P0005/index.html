Ankle Bending and Rotation

Ankle Bending and Rotation (Goolf Naman Chakra)

A combined sequence integrating ankle dorsiflexion/plantarflexion (P0003) with full circumduction (P0004) into a single continuous practice. The combined form is typically used in standard IAYT protocols as the definitive ankle preparation exercise when time does not permit the full individual sequence.

1. Technique

Starting position (Sthiti): Dandasana (seated with legs extended) or Supine Posture.

Practice:

  1. Begin with 5 rounds of Ankle Bending — dorsiflexion and plantarflexion (as in P0003).
  2. Without pause, transition to 10 rounds of clockwise Ankle Rotation (as in P0004).
  3. Reverse: 10 rounds anti-clockwise rotation.
  4. Complete with 5 more rounds of Ankle Bending to return to the linear axis.
  5. Relax.

Breathing: Normal throughout. The transition from bending to rotation is seamless.

Duration: Approximately 2–3 minutes for the complete combined sequence.

2. Anatomical Activation

Combines all structures engaged in P0003 and P0004:

  • Sagittal plane muscles: tibialis anterior, gastrocnemius, soleus
  • Frontal plane muscles: peroneus longus/brevis (eversion); tibialis posterior (inversion)
  • All ankle joint surfaces: talocrural, subtalar, midtarsal
  • Calf venous pump: activated through the plantarflexion component

3. Physiological Effects

The combined practice provides all effects described individually in P0003 and P0004:

  • Venous pump activation from the bending component
  • Complete articular surface lubrication from the rotation component
  • Peroneal proprioceptive retraining from the circumduction
  • Retinacular mobilisation across all planes

The combination is more than the sum of its parts: the transition from linear to circular movement challenges the neuromuscular coordination of the ankle — requiring smooth, coordinated activation of all four compartment muscle groups in sequence. This coordination training is distinct from either exercise performed in isolation.

Clinical use as standard protocol exercise

In IAYT protocols where time is limited, the combined form replaces both P0003 and P0004 — providing comprehensive ankle preparation in a single continuous sequence. This is the version most commonly taught in clinical yoga therapy settings.

4. Kosha Level

Primary: Annamaya Kosha — complete ankle joint system mobilisation, covering all planes of motion in a single integrated practice.

Secondary: Pranamaya Kosha — the continuous circular flow of the combined practice is understood as a complete prana circuit through the ankle nadis.

5. Indicated For

Condition Reasoning
Arthritis — Ankle (A003) Comprehensive ankle preparation combining venous return and full articular mobilisation
Varicose Veins Calf pump component provides venous return benefit
Chronic Ankle Instability Peroneal retraining through circumduction component
All IAYT protocols (preparatory) Standard combined ankle exercise — used when full separate sequence is not performed
Diabetes (A001) Peripheral circulation improvement; sensorimotor reactivation

6. Contraindications

  • Acute ankle injury — same as P0003 and P0004
  • Acute ankle arthritis with effusion — reduce range during acute phase

7. Variations & Progressions

Shortened (clinical protocol standard): 5 bending + 5 rotation each direction — approximately 90 seconds total.

Extended (dedicated ankle focus): 10 bending + 10 rotation each direction + 10 bending — used when ankle mobility is the primary therapeutic target.

Sequencing context: This combined form typically replaces the individual P0003 and P0004 in standard IAYT protocols. Follows P0001–P0002 (toe mobilisation). Progresses to P0006 (Patella Movement / kneecap series).
